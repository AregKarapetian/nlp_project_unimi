In the grand kingdom of Azura, where the sun dipped into the horizon and painted the sky with hues of crimson and gold, King Alfonso ruled with wisdom and justice. He had three sons, Pedro, Diego, and Juan, who were as dear to him as the stars in the night sky. One day, the king summoned his sons and bade them set out from the kingdom to seek their fortunes, but first they must agree on a meeting place in the forest. After many days of walking, Don Juan chanced upon an old man on the road, who handed him bread and instructed him to divide it among the monkeys guarding the palace gate. Don Juan did as he was told and entered the palace, where he met Chonguita, the beautiful daughter of the monkey king.

The archbishop of the monkeys performed a grand ceremony, uniting Don Juan and Chonguita in matrimony. A few days later, Don Juan requested permission from his wife to visit their brothers at the agreed meeting place. Chonguita's mother, the queen of the monkeys, insisted that her daughter accompany Don Juan on this journey. Though Don Juan was mortified by the prospect of traveling with a monkey as his companion, he was forced to comply.

When they arrived at the appointed place, Don Juan's brothers and their wives were shocked to behold Chonguita, but Don Juan could not bring himself to speak a word in her defense. The king, upon learning of his youngest son's union with a monkey, fainted from shock, but eventually accepted it as God's will.

To test the skills of his daughters-in-law, the king commanded each of them to create an embroidered coat within three days under penalty of death. Chonguita, despite being a monkey, proved herself to be a skilled needlewoman and crafted the most exquisite coat of all. The king, still determined to rid himself of the monkey wife, ordered his daughters-in-law to embroider caps for him in two days.

Finally, the king devised one last test: he summoned his daughters-in-law and challenged them to draw the prettiest picture on the walls of his chamber within three days. Chonguita's artwork surpassed that of her sisters-in-law, and Don Juan was crowned king in a grand ceremony. As they celebrated in the palace, Don Juan became enraged with Chonguita for insisting he dance with her, and he hurled her against the wall.

In an instant, the hall plunged into darkness, but soon brightened once more, revealing that Chonguita had been transformed into a beautiful woman. The king's courtiers whispered among themselves, marveling at the magic of the forest, where even the most unlikely creatures could possess hidden talents and virtues.

Moral: Even in the face of adversity, patience and acceptance can reveal unexpected blessings.

In days of yore, when the great sea stretched far and wide, a mighty dragon dwelled within its depths. His beloved wife, whose beauty shone like the morning sun, began to wither away, her radiance fading like a flower in autumn's chill. The dragon, filled with love and concern for his dear one, asked, "My dear, what shall I bring you to eat? Your color is not good; tell me, that I may fetch it for thee." But the dragon's wife remained silent, her eyes cast downward.

The dragon pleaded with her, "Trust me, my love. I will get whatever your heart desires. Tell me, and I shall hasten to bring it back to thee." The dragon's wife finally spoke, her voice barely above a whisper, "I desire the heart of a monkey, that I may eat and regain my strength."

The dragon set off towards the mountain forests, where the monkeys dwelled. He spied a clever primate perched high in a tree and called out to him, "Hail, shining one! Are you not afraid of falling from this lofty height?" The monkey replied with a grin, "Fear? Ha! I have no such fear." The dragon said, "Why eat of one tree alone? Cross the sea with me, and thou shalt find forests of fruit and flowers beyond thy wildest dreams."

The dragon offered to carry the monkey on his back, and they set off across the waves. But as they reached the midpoint, the dragon dived down into the depths of the ocean. The monkey's eyes grew salty from the sea spray, and he cried out in alarm, "Where are you taking me?" The dragon replied with a chuckle, "Oh, dear sir! My wife is ill and has taken a fancy to thy heart." The monkey thought quickly and said, "Illustrious friend, why didst thou not tell me sooner? I left my heart on the top of this tree; take me back, that I may retrieve it for thy beloved wife."

The dragon returned to the shore, and as the monkey tarried in climbing down from his perch, he exclaimed, "Hurry up, little friend! I am waiting!" The monkey thought within himself, "What a fool this dragon is!" And thus, the great Buddha revealed to his followers, "At that very moment, I was the clever monkey."

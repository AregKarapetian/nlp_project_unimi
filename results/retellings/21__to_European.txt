In days of yore, in the vast expanse of the ocean, dwelt a mighty dragon with a loving heart. His wife, fair and gentle as a summer breeze, lay ill and pale. The dragon, beset with worry, asked his dear one, "My love, what shall I bring to ease your suffering?" But she remained silent, her eyes cast downward.

"Tell me, my dear," the dragon pleaded, "and I shall fetch it for you." His wife's voice was barely audible as she whispered, "A monkey's heart... I desire a monkey's heart."

The dragon's brow furrowed in concern. "Alas, fair one, the monkeys dwell in the mountain forests! How can I obtain their hearts?" But his wife merely shook her head and said, "I am soon to depart this world. What matters it?"

Undeterred, the dragon set off for the mountains, his heart heavy with love. He spied a mischievous monkey perched atop a towering tree and called out, "Greetings, noble creature! Do you not fear falling from your lofty perch?" The monkey replied with a grin, "Fear? Ha! I am as free as the wind."

The dragon proposed, "Why confine yourself to this single tree? Cross the sea and discover forests of fruit and flowers!" The monkey's eyes sparkled with curiosity. "But how shall I cross?" he asked. The dragon offered his back, saying, "Climb upon my shoulders, little one." As they set off across the waves, the dragon dived beneath the surface.

The monkey cried out in alarm, "Where are you taking me?" The dragon replied with a chuckle, "My dear sir, my wife is ailing and has taken a fancy to your heart. Alas, I must retrieve it for her." The monkey thought to himself, "Foolish dragon! Why did he not speak of this sooner?"

The monkey returned to the tree, only to find that his heart was still perched upon its branch. He called out to the dragon below, "Wait, noble friend! I left my heart here; take me back and I shall fetch it for your wife." The dragon waited patiently as the monkey descended from the tree.

As they parted ways, the monkey thought to himself, "What a simple-minded creature this dragon is!" And thus, in that very moment, the great Buddha revealed his true form: he was the mischievous monkey.

In a desolate wasteland, a humble hermit chanced upon an enormous cave hidden from view. Weary from his journey, he entered the cave to rest and was immediately struck by the radiant glow emanating from within. The cave's interior was filled with an abundance of gold, shining brightly like the sun.

Without hesitation, the hermit fled the cave as if pursued by a malevolent spirit. His frantic pace soon caught the attention of three nefarious bandits who had been lurking in the area, preying on unsuspecting travelers. They were intrigued by the hermit's hasty departure and decided to investigate further.

As they lay in wait, the hermit stumbled upon them and revealed that he was fleeing from Death itself. The bandits, skeptical at first, demanded to see this "Death" for themselves. The hermit, fearing their intentions, reluctantly led them back to the cave.

Upon entering the cave once more, the bandits were awestruck by the treasure trove of gold before them. They congratulated each other on their good fortune and dismissed the hermit, sending him on his way.

The three bandits remained behind, bickering over what to do with their newfound wealth. One suggested they leave it untouched, while another proposed dividing some of it among themselves and using the rest for supplies. A cunning third bandit, however, had other plans.

In secret, he confided in himself that he would poison his companions' food, allowing him to claim the treasure for himself once they were dead. As fate would have it, this treacherous plan was set in motion.

The three bandits agreed to send one of their number to a nearby city to purchase provisions and supplies. The chosen bandit, with a heart full of malice, took advantage of his opportunity to carry out his nefarious scheme.

Upon returning from the city, he brought with him poisoned food, which he shared among his companions. Unbeknownst to them, their own greed had led to their downfall. As they devoured the tainted victuals, they succumbed to its deadly effects, one by one.

In the end, all three bandits met a gruesome fate, slain by their own hand in a fit of jealousy and greed. The treasure remained untouched, a testament to the wisdom of the sage hermit who had fled Death's pursuit, leaving the gold to remain as it was – unspoiled and free from harm.

As the wind whispered through the desolate wasteland, a lesson was learned: that those who seek wealth at any cost shall ultimately find only their own demise. The hermit, wise in his decision to flee, had escaped the fate of the three bandits, whose greed and treachery led them down a path from which there was no return.

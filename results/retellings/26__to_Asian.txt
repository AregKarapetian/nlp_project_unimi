In a time of great drought, when rivers dried up and springs ran dry, the creatures of the land gathered to discuss the crisis. A wise old Elephant proposed that they dance on the dry riverbed to bring forth water from the depths. The other animals agreed, but one skeptical Rabbit refused to join in.

The others danced with all their might, stomping and leaping until the earth trembled and water began to flow. They rejoiced at their success, but Rabbit scoffed at them, saying he would drink their water regardless of their efforts. That evening, he snuck back to the riverbed and drank his fill.

The next morning, the animals discovered Rabbit's footprints in the mud, and they knew he had been there. A great commotion arose as they tried to decide how to catch the cunning Rabbit. Many suggestions were made, but none seemed feasible until old Tortoise spoke up.

"I will catch him," said Tortoise, with a slow and deliberate nod. "Rub my shell with pitch from the black beeswax tree, and I will lie down at the river's edge, blending in with the stones. When Rabbit steps on me, his feet will stick fast."

The animals agreed to try Tortoise's plan, and soon his shell was covered in a thick layer of pitch. He lay down carefully, drawing his head into his shell, and waited for Rabbit to arrive.

As night fell, Rabbit came to drink from the river, unaware of the trap set before him. "Ha!" he chuckled, spotting what he thought was just another stone. "They're being kind, placing a convenient stepping stone here." He trod on Tortoise with his left foot, and it stuck fast in the pitch.

Tortoise slowly emerged from his shell, and Rabbit tried to free himself by kicking with his right forefoot. But it too became stuck, as did his hind foot when he tried to stomp down. Frustrated, Rabbit used his head to hammer Tortoise's shell, but even that met the same fate.

Tortoise then began to move slowly back towards the other animals, carrying Rabbit on his back. The creatures cheered and jeered at Rabbit's predicament, calling out for him to admit defeat.

But they soon realized they had to decide what to do with the cunning rabbit. Some suggested beheading or severe punishment, but Rabbit begged them not to inflict a shameful death upon him. "Take me by my tail and dash my head against a stone," he pleaded, his voice trembling.

The animals agreed, thinking it would be a fitting end for the rebellious rabbit. But who should carry out the task? Lion, with his mighty strength, was chosen for the duty. He walked forward, Rabbit struggling in his grasp, but just as all seemed lost for the rabbit, something unexpected happened.

As Lion took Rabbit by the tail and swung him around, the white skin on Rabbit's back began to slip off, revealing a coat of brilliant white fur underneath. The animals gasped in surprise as they realized that Rabbit was not what he seemed. In fact, he was Chonguita, a master of disguise and deception.

The creatures cheered and clapped for Chonguita's cunning and skill, and from that day on, he was welcomed into their community as a hero and a friend.

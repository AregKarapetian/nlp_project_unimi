In days of yore, when darkness descended upon the mountains like a shroud, a poor wight chanced to find himself lost and alone. Seeking refuge from the tempests that howled through the trees, he stumbled upon a hollow tree, its trunk a vast and ancient vessel that swallowed him whole.

As he huddled in the darkness, a merry band of elves gathered at the tree's base, their laughter and music echoing through the night like a chorus of woodland sprites. The man, peering out from his hiding place, was taken aback by their revelry, but as the hours passed, their merriment proved contagious. He crept out of his sanctuary, drawn into the circle of dancers and musicians, where he laughed and sang with abandon.

When dawn's first light began to creep over the horizon, the elves bid him farewell, saying, "You are a true companion in joy, and we would have you join us again. Make us a promise, and keep it, that you may return to our company." And so they took from his forehead a wen, a blemish that had long marred his visage, as security for his pledge.

The man, overjoyed at having escaped the darkness and gained a new lease on life, made his way back to his village, where he regaled his friends with tales of his wild adventure. They congratulated him warmly, celebrating his newfound freedom from the wen.

But not all were pleased by his good fortune. A neighbor, consumed by envy, heard of the man's luck and set out to find the hollow tree for himself. He tracked it down, and when he joined the elves' revelry, they welcomed him with open arms, saying, "You have kept your promise, and we shall return what is yours." And so they restored the wen to his forehead, adding it to the one that already grew there.

The envious neighbor returned home in tears, his face now a map of wens, a reminder to all who would listen of the perils of coveting another's good fortune.

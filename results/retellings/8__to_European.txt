In days of yore, when darkness descended upon the mountains like a shroud, a certain man found himself forced to seek refuge in the hollow trunk of an ancient tree. As he huddled within its depths, a band of mischievous elves gathered 'neath the branches, their laughter and music carrying on the night air.

The man's initial fright gave way to curiosity as the elves' revelry continued unabated. Entranced by their merriment, he crept from his hiding place to join in their festivities. The elves welcomed him with open arms, and together they danced beneath the starry sky until the first light of dawn.

As the night wore on, the elves presented the man with a challenge: to prove himself worthy of their company, he must compete against them in three trials. First, he would don a coat woven from the finest spider silk; next, he would don a cap adorned with precious gems; and finally, he would create a masterpiece that would rival the greatest works of art.

The man, though initially hesitant, accepted the challenge. To his surprise, he proved himself to be the equal of the elves in each trial. His coat shone like the sun, his cap sparkled like diamonds, and his artwork surpassed even the most skilled artisans.

The elves, impressed by his prowess, declared him the winner of their games. As a reward, they gifted him with a magical token that would bring him good fortune for all eternity. And so, the man returned to his village, hailed as a hero by all who knew him.

But not everyone was pleased by the man's success. A neighboring villager, consumed by envy and discontent, sought to replicate the man's luck for himself. He stumbled upon the hollow tree and, under the guise of being the man's friend, convinced the elves to return his "pledge." Unbeknownst to him, however, the elves had no intention of releasing their token so easily.

With a mischievous grin, one of the elves presented the villager with a wen that grew on his forehead – only to stick it onto the top of another wen he already bore. The envious neighbor returned home in tears, his envy and discontent now matched by the physical burden of two wens. And so, the villagers learned a valuable lesson about the dangers of coveting what others possess.

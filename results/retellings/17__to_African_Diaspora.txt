In a land far and wide, where the sun dipped into the horizon like a fiery blade, there lived a cunning lion with eyes as sharp as the morning dew. He had grown tired of his life in the savannah, so he devised a plan to escape. But alas, he was trapped in a rocky cleft, unable to free himself.

A wise and gentle horse, with a coat as black as the night sky, overheard the lion's plea for help. "I'll do that," said the horse, "but you must promise not to eat me." The lion, with a tongue as smooth as honey, promised to spare the horse's life. And so, the horse dug with his hooves until he had freed the lion.

But as soon as the lion was free, he forgot his promise and said, "Now I am going to eat you!" The horse, with a heart full of wisdom, replied, "What were the conditions? Did we not agree that you would not eat me?" The lion, with a temper as fierce as the wind, shouted back, "That makes no difference now!"

The horse, however, was not one to be intimidated. He said, "But if you want to, we can go before a judge." And so they went to see the wise and cunning fox, who sat under a baobab tree, his eyes gleaming with wisdom.

The lion presented the question to the fox, but he refused to give a verdict until he had seen how the two of them were standing. So all three of them went to the rocky cleft, where the horse positioned himself in the same place he had stood when he helped the lion. The fox then told the lion to force himself back into the cleft, just as he was before.

The lion pushed and pushed until he was stuck fast, unable to get out again. "So," said the fox, "now you are exactly where you were before. Now the horse can decide whether he will help you out again." But the horse did not want to be fooled twice. He threw stones at the lion until he was dead.

As the sun rose over the savannah, the people gathered around and said, "Yes indeed, the fox is sly! And the horse is wise beyond measure!"

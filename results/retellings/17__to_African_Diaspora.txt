In a land far and wide, where savannas stretched as far as the eye could see, there lived a mighty lion with a heart as fierce as the sun. His mane stood tall like a crown, and his roar thundered through the grasslands. One day, while exploring the rocky outcrops, he found himself trapped in a narrow cleft.

"Ah, wretched fate!" cried the lion. "Who will set me free?" Just then, a wise horse with a coat as black as coal appeared on the scene. The lion begged for help, promising not to devour him once freed.

The horse agreed, but only after extracting a promise from the lion. With his powerful hooves, he dug and dug until the lion was finally set free. But as soon as the lion's paws touched solid ground, he snarled, "Now I'll eat you!"

"What were our conditions?" asked the horse calmly, like a wise elder. The lion sneered, but the horse stood firm: "Did we not agree that you would spare my life?"

The two animals went before a wise fox, who listened carefully to their tale. "Let me see how you both stood," said the fox, his eyes twinkling with cunning. They returned to the rocky cleft, and the lion positioned himself as he had before.

But the fox was not fooled. He asked the lion to push himself in tighter, just as he had been when begging for help. The lion complied, but each time he pushed in a little more, his front leg went further inside. Finally, he was wedged in so tightly that he couldn't escape.

The fox chuckled and said, "Now you're exactly where you were before! Will the horse still help you out?" But the horse had no intention of doing so. Instead, he hurled stones at the lion until he lay lifeless on the ground.

"Ah," said the wise old fox with a knowing smile, "sometimes justice is served in unexpected ways."

In days of yore, when the sun shone bright and the forest was alive with creatures great and small, Brer Rabbit wandered through the underbrush, singing a merry tune to himself. As he strolled along, a shadow flitted across his path, and he looked up to behold Mr. Hawk soaring overhead. The hawk's wings beat steadily as he circled closer and closer, until Brer Rabbit felt the rush of air from his wings and suddenly found himself snatched from the ground.

Mr. Hawk held him fast in his talons, squeezing tight enough to make Brer Rabbit's breath come short. He cried out for mercy, but Mr. Hawk would not be swayed. Brer Rabbit begged and pleaded, but it was all in vain. Just when it seemed as though all hope was lost, he lay still and pondered his predicament.

Presently, an idea struck him, and he spoke up, "I know not what you desire of me, Mr. Hawk. I am hardly worth the trouble of catching." Mr. Hawk replied, "I shall dispatch you forthwith, and then I shall set my sights on a pair of jay-birds that have been causing quite the commotion in these woods."

Brer Rabbit's eyes grew wide with alarm at the mention of those detestable birds, and he exclaimed, "Oh dear Mr. Hawk, please do go catch them first! Their very presence grates upon my nerves." Mr. Hawk's expression turned skeptical, and he said, "You've been known to deceive many a creature, Brer Rabbit. I fear you're not fooling me this time."

Brer Rabbit thought quickly and replied, "If that be the case, then perhaps it would be best for you to wait until I have grown tame. For now, I am far too wild to make for a satisfying meal." Mr. Hawk's eyes narrowed, but Brer Rabbit pressed on, saying, "And if even that does not suffice, then let me grow big and strong, so that I may provide a full and satisfying feast."

Mr. Hawk seemed taken aback by this proposal, and Brer Rabbit continued to beg and plead until the hawk finally relented. And so, with Mr. Hawk distracted, Brer Rabbit managed to wriggle free from his grasp and make good his escape. He did not grow big or strong, nor did he drive out any partridges for Mr. Hawk's benefit. In the end, it was Brer Rabbit who emerged victorious, having outwitted the cunning hawk once again.

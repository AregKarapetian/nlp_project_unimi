In a far-off kingdom, there lived a wise and just Sultan who had a beautiful daughter, blessed with a gentle heart and a thirst for knowledge. The Sultan entrusted his daughter's education to a respected priest, instructing him to teach her the sacred teachings of the Koran.

However, the priest's intentions were not pure. He harbored secret desires for the young princess, but she refused to yield to his advances. One day, she cleverly outwitted him by pretending to invite him to her chambers, only to secretly remove the ladder that led to her quarters.

The following day, the priest sent a deceitful letter to the Sultan, falsely accusing his daughter of immorality. The Sultan, gullible and enraged, ordered his loyal guards to bring the princess before him. He condemned her to death, intending to have her throat cut by the slaves who were tasked with carrying out the sentence.

As the slaves led the young woman to a secluded wooded area, they cruelly amputated her legs while digging her grave. But fate had other plans. As she lay helpless, she miraculously crawled away and hid among the trees. The slaves, convinced that they had carried out their duty, returned to the palace with a bottle of gazelle's blood, claiming it was the princess's lifeblood.

Days passed, but the Sultan remained unaware of his daughter's fate. Meanwhile, a caravan happened upon the wooded area, where they discovered the young woman sitting under a tree, her legs miraculously restored. A kind-hearted traveler took pity on her and brought her to his home in a nearby town.

Later, the Sultan's son chanced upon the young woman while visiting the town. He was struck by her beauty and requested that she be given to him as his wife, despite being informed of her physical disability. The man who had taken her in agreed, and they were married.

As time passed, the young couple welcomed two children into their family. When the Sultan's son departed on a pilgrimage, leaving his wife behind with a ram, he entrusted her with the care of their home. During her husband's absence, she had a prophetic dream in which two birds perched upon her restored legs.

Upon waking, she discovered that her legs had indeed grown back, and she was able to walk freely once more. With her children and the ram by her side, she set out on a journey to join her husband on pilgrimage. Along the way, they encountered various travelers, including her father, brother, and the deceitful priest who had wronged her.

As they neared their destination, the Sultan recognized his daughter and was overcome with emotion. He listened in awe as she recounted her tale of woe and resilience. Moved by her story, he ordered the execution of the treacherous priest, restoring balance to their family.

In a joyous reunion, the Sultan, his daughter, and her husband continued on their pilgrimage together, their bond strengthened by the trials they had faced. From that day forward, the young couple's love flourished, and their children grew up in a household filled with compassion, forgiveness, and wisdom.

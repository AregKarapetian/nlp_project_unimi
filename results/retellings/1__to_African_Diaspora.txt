In the village of Azul, where the sun dips into the horizon and paints the sky with hues of crimson and gold, there lived a king with three sons: Pedro, Diego, and Juan. The king, wise and just, called his sons to him one day and said, "My children, I have a task for you. Go forth from our kingdom and seek your fortunes. May the road rise up to meet you, and may the wind be at your back."

The three brothers set out on their journey, each taking a different path. But before they parted ways, they agreed to meet in a certain place deep within the forest. After many days of walking, Don Juan came upon an old man sitting by the side of the road. The old man offered Don Juan bread and said, "Take this gift from me, but be warned: as you enter the palace ahead, divide the bread among the monkeys guarding the gate. Otherwise, you will not be able to pass."

Don Juan took the bread and followed the old man's instructions. As he entered the palace, a large monkey approached him. Don Juan was taken aback, but the monkey spoke to him in a gentle voice, saying, "Don Juan, I know that your heart is pure and your intentions are good. At this very moment, my daughter Chonguita will marry you."

The archbishop of the monkeys was summoned, and Don Juan and Chonguita were married without delay. A few days later, Don Juan asked permission from his wife to visit the place where he and his brothers had agreed to meet. Chonguita's mother, however, urged her daughter to go with him, saying, "If you are going away, take Chonguita with you."

Don Juan was ashamed to travel with a monkey as his companion, but he had no choice. Together, they set out for the forest, where Don Pedro and Don Diego awaited them with their wives. When Don Juan arrived, his brothers were surprised to see him accompanied by a monkey.

"What has happened to you?" asked Don Pedro. "Did you lose your head?"

Don Juan could not speak, but he knew that he had to think quickly. He said, "Let us go home! Our father must be waiting for us." And so they began their journey back to the kingdom.

When the king learned of his son's marriage to a monkey, he was taken aback. But he said to himself, "This misfortune is God's will. I must bear it with patience."

The king assigned a house to each couple and ordered them to make him an embroidered coat within three days. The king hoped that Chonguita would fail, but she surprised everyone by creating the most beautiful coat of all.

Undeterred, the king issued another challenge: to embroider a cap for him within two days under penalty of death. Again, Chonguita's work was the most exquisite of all.

Finally, the king summoned his daughters-in-law and said, "The husband of the one who can draw the prettiest picture on the walls of my chamber shall succeed me on the throne." The three couples worked tirelessly for three days, but when the king inspected their work, he found that Chonguita's picture was by far the most beautiful.

And so Don Juan was crowned king. A grand feast was held in his honor, with music and dancing filling the palace halls. But as the night wore on, Don Juan became angry with his wife for insisting that he dance with her. In a fit of rage, he hurled her against the wall.

The hall grew dark, but soon it brightened again, and Chonguita had been transformed into a beautiful woman. The king's courtiers gasped in amazement as they beheld the new queen, radiant and lovely beyond measure.

From that day on, Don Juan ruled with wisdom and justice, and his kingdom prospered under his reign. And Chonguita, now a true queen, stood by her husband's side, a shining example of the power of love and transformation.

As the villagers say, "A seed planted in good soil will grow into a mighty tree." Don Juan's story is a testament to this proverb, reminding us that even the most unlikely of circumstances can lead to greatness.

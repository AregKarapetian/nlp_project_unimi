In the land of our ancestors, where the sun dips into the horizon and paints the sky with hues of crimson and gold, there lived a mighty king who possessed a gift from the gods – the ability to converse with animals. He had spent many moons studying their language, and now he could understand their chirps, chattering, and even their most subtle whispers.

One day, as the king was strolling through his gardens, he overheard a group of birds discussing his benevolence towards them. "Our king is kind to us," they said in unison. "He provides for our needs, and we are grateful." The king's face lit up with joy, and he laughed at their words.

His queen, who was standing nearby, noticed her husband's merriment and asked, "What has brought you such delight?" The king hesitated, knowing that if he revealed the truth – that he had understood the birds' conversation – his life would be forfeit. So, with a mischievous glint in his eye, he replied, "I merely laughed."

The queen's curiosity was piqued, and she pressed him for an explanation. The king, however, remained tight-lipped, fearing that if he revealed the secret of animal language, it would be his undoing.

As the days passed, the queen continued to prod her husband, insisting that there must be a reason behind his laughter. The king, feeling trapped by her persistence, finally relented and decided to confide in the animals themselves.

He approached them with a mixture of trepidation and amusement, knowing that they would soon discover his secret. And indeed, as he watched from afar, the animals began to whisper among themselves about the king's impending demise.

A wise old cock, who had lived for many seasons, stepped forward and said, "Our king is doomed. We shall not receive his assistance anymore. Unless he perishes, we too will suffer." The king listened intently as the cock continued, "I have many hens under my care. When I call them, they come; when I tell them to eat, they eat; and when I instruct them to depart, they go."

The king's laughter echoed through the land once more, but this time it was tinged with a hint of sadness. His queen asked again what had caused his mirth, and he replied, "I was contemplating building a tank." The queen, convinced that her husband was indeed planning such a project, urged him to bring the animals together to begin construction.

The king, pretending to agree, gathered the animals and led them to a spot where they could supposedly build the tank. However, as soon as he left their presence, the animals realized that they were being tricked. They began to struggle with the earth, but their efforts were in vain.

A cunning jackal, who had been observing from afar, bounded away, leaving the others to their futile labor. The king, returning to inspect their progress, noticed the jackal's absence and asked him why he was not contributing to the task.

The jackal, with a sly grin on his face, replied, "I am merely calculating the balance of our society." The king, intrigued by this response, pressed the jackal for further explanation. "Are the females in excess or the males?" he asked.

The jackal thought for a moment before responding, "From what I have observed, the females are indeed in excess." But when the king returned home to verify this claim, he discovered that the opposite was true – the males outnumbered the females.

The king's eyes widened as he realized his mistake. He called the jackal and said, "Bola, you were correct. The males are in excess." The jackal smiled knowingly and said, "Your Majesty, I counted not only the females but also those who listen to their words – the males."

The king, impressed by the jackal's wisdom, decided to test his understanding further. He asked the animals if they could build a tank, and when they replied that it was impossible for them to carry earth, he sent them back into the forest.

As the queen inquired about the progress of the tank, the king turned on her with a cane, beating her mercilessly. The queen, realizing that she had been outwitted by the cunning jackal, vowed never to speak or ask anything again, weeping tears of shame and sorrow.

And so, the mighty king learned a valuable lesson from the wise jackal – that sometimes, even the most seemingly foolish creatures possess hidden wisdom and insight.

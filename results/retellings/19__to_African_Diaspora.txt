In a small village nestled between two great rivers, there lived a mighty king who possessed a special gift – he could understand the language of animals. One day, as he sat on his throne, surrounded by his courtiers and advisors, the birds perched outside his window began to chatter among themselves.

"Ah, our beloved king is so kind to us," they said. "He provides for our needs and protects us from harm."

The king overheard their conversation and couldn't help but chuckle at their praise. His queen, who was seated beside him, noticed her husband's amusement and asked, "What has brought you such joy, my dear?"

The king hesitated, knowing that if he revealed the secret of understanding animal language, it would put his life in danger. He replied, "I merely laughed," trying to brush off the question.

But the queen was not one to be easily satisfied. She pressed him further, saying, "There must be a reason for your laughter. If you don't tell me, I fear that something terrible will happen."

The king's resolve weakened under her persistence, and he finally decided to reveal the secret. He went out into the courtyard, where the animals were gathered, and announced his intention to share their language with them.

However, a wise old cock who had been watching the scene unfold spoke up, saying, "Your Majesty, we don't want your charity. We are capable of taking care of ourselves. In fact, if you were to die, it would be for the best. For then, we could roam freely and live without fear of your rule."

The king laughed at the cock's bold words, but his queen was not amused. She demanded that he explain why he found the cock's statement so amusing.

The king tried to think quickly and came up with a new excuse, saying, "I was laughing because I had an idea for building a grand tank in our kingdom."

His queen, convinced by his explanation, ordered the animals to be brought to the site where the tank would be built. The king, accompanied by the animals, showed them the location and instructed them to begin construction.

However, as the days passed, it became clear that the animals were not capable of building a tank. They struggled with moving earth, and their efforts only resulted in chaos. A clever jackal, who had been watching from the sidelines, approached the king and offered an explanation for the animals' failure.

"I was looking at the balance of nature," said the jackal. "From what I've observed, there are more females than males in our kingdom."

The king, intrigued by the jackal's claim, asked him to explain further. The jackal pointed out that if one counted all the males who listened to and obeyed their female counterparts, then indeed, the females would be in excess.

The king was impressed by the jackal's wisdom and decided to investigate further. He returned home and consulted his records, only to discover that the jackal had been correct – there were more males than females in the kingdom.

When he confronted the jackal with this information, the cunning animal revealed another clever point. "Your Majesty," said the jackal, "you must consider not just the physical count of males and females but also their roles in society."

The king was taken aback by the jackal's insight and realized that his queen had been right all along – he did possess a special gift, one that allowed him to understand the complexities of nature. He thanked the jackal for his wisdom and sent him away with a reward.

As the king returned home, he found his queen waiting for him, inquiring about the progress of the tank's construction. But instead of answering her question, he took up his cane and began to beat her, saying, "You have been trying to teach me a lesson all along, haven't you?"

The queen, realizing that she had underestimated the king's wisdom, cried out in distress, acknowledging that she would never again try to manipulate him. The king, satisfied with having learned a valuable lesson from the jackal, let his wife go and continued to rule his kingdom with a newfound understanding of the balance between nature and human society.

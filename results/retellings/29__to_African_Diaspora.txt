In the heart of the desert, where the sun beats down relentlessly, there lived a wise hermit who roamed the dunes with purpose. One day, while traversing the arid expanse, he stumbled upon a cave hidden from view by the shifting sands. Weary from his journey, he decided to rest within its cool depths.

As soon as his eyes adjusted to the darkness, they fell upon a glint of gold that shone brightly in one corner. The hermit's heart skipped a beat, but instead of succumbing to temptation, he turned and fled with all haste. His feet pounded against the sand as he hastened away from the cave.

Meanwhile, three rough men lurked nearby, preying upon travelers who dared to traverse their domain. They had never laid eyes on the gold within the cave, nor did they suspect its presence. As they watched the hermit flee without pursuit, they exchanged curious glances.

"Brothers," one of them called out, "what drives this man to flee so precipitously?" The hermit, still running, replied over his shoulder, "Death chases me, and I dare not look back."

The three men were perplexed by the hermit's words, but their curiosity got the better of them. They asked him to lead them to whatever was chasing him, and the hermit reluctantly obliged.

As they approached the cave, he warned them, "Do not follow me into this place, for I fear what lies within." But his words fell on deaf ears, and they followed him into the cave's depths.

There, in the corner where the gold shone brightly, the men saw nothing but a pile of glittering metal. They laughed and congratulated each other on their good fortune, dismissing the hermit with a wave of their hands.

As he departed, they began to discuss how to divide the treasure among themselves. One suggested that they stay and guard it until they could carry it all away; another proposed sending one of them to the city to sell some of the gold and bring back supplies.

The devil, who often whispers evil counsel into men's ears, had already begun to work his mischief. He had planted a wicked thought in the mind of the man chosen to go to the city: "When I am alone, I will eat and drink my fill, and then poison what I bring back to my companions, so that when they are dead, I shall be the sole owner of this wealth."

The man, unaware of the devil's influence, set off for the city with a heart full of greed. Meanwhile, his two companions waited patiently in the cave, their minds consumed by thoughts of betrayal.

As soon as the poisoned food was brought back to the cave, they fell upon it with glee, devouring what they believed would be their share of the treasure. But little did they know that death had already been invited into their midst.

The three men ate and drank until they were sated, but their satisfaction turned to despair as the poison took hold. They died in each other's arms, their greed and treachery ultimately leading them to a fate worse than death itself.

And so, the gold remained untouched, its secret safe from those who would have sought to claim it for themselves. The hermit, wise to the ways of the world, had fled the cave unscathed, leaving behind those who had succumbed to their own darkness.

As the sun dipped below the horizon, casting the desert in a warm orange glow, the people of the nearby village whispered among themselves about the fate that had befallen the three men. They spoke of how traitors often reap what they sow, and how the Lord God rewards those who walk in righteousness.

The hermit, now far away from the cave, smiled to himself as he pondered the lesson of the desert sands: that true wisdom lies not in accumulating wealth, but in living a life guided by virtue and compassion.

In a small village nestled in the mountains, there lived three brothers who were as different as the seasons that came and went. Two of them wore tattered clothes, while the youngest had none at all. The weather was treacherous, with dark clouds gathering on the horizon like an omen. Determined to bring back some meat for their family's dinner, they set out to go hunting.

As they walked through the forest, they stumbled upon a hare, its fur glistening in the fading light. But alas, their guns were no match for the swift creature, and it escaped into the underbrush. The brothers looked at each other in frustration, wondering what to do next.

Suddenly, one of them remembered that their wise godmother lived nearby. They hastened to her doorstep, where they knocked loudly until she answered from within. "My children," she said, "what brings you here?" They explained their situation, and she offered to lend them a pot to cook the hare they had not caught.

The brothers thanked her and went into the kitchen, where they found three pots hanging from hooks on the wall. Two were broken and one had no bottom, but Chonguita, the youngest brother, chose it without hesitation. They put the hare inside and set it over the fire to cook.

As the sun began to set, one of the brothers suggested that they ask their godmother about her garden. She told them she had three walnut trees, two of which were dead and one that had never borne any nuts. Chonguita went to shake the barren tree, but a nut fell onto his hat and broke his heel.

The brothers picked up some nuts and returned to get the hare, which was now cooked to perfection. They decided to take it to the village nearby, where many people were ill and in need of nourishment. Chonguita put up a notice on the street that whoever wanted broth could come to a certain place and receive it for free.

Many villagers came, but one stranger drank so much of the broth that he nearly died from overindulgence. The brothers then sought out three physicians who lived in the village – one was blind, one deaf, and one dumb. Chonguita went to see them, and they each tried to diagnose his condition.

The blind physician asked to examine his tongue, but Chonguita just smiled and said nothing. The deaf physician asked how he was feeling, but Chonguita simply shook his head. The dumb physician asked for paper, pen, and ink to write a prescription, but Chonguita just chuckled and handed him the instruments.

The dumb physician scribbled something on the paper and gave it to Chonguita, who read it aloud: "Go to the apothecary and buy two cents' worth of medicine. Put it wherever you wish, and he will recover when he can." The brothers laughed at the absurdity of it all, but they decided to follow the physician's advice.

They went to the apothecary, who sold them a small packet of mysterious powder for two cents. They put it in their bag and returned home, where their family was waiting with bated breath. Chonguita's cleverness had saved the day once again, and his brothers looked up to him with newfound admiration.

From that day on, Chonguita was known throughout the village as a master of wit and cunning, able to outsmart even the most absurd challenges that came his way.

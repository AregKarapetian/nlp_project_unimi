In a small village nestled between two great mountains, there lived three brothers who were as poor as the poorest of beggars. Two had not even a stitch to their backs, while the third wore only a tattered shirt. The weather was as fierce as a raging tiger, and the brothers decided to go hunting for food.

They gathered three ancient guns from the attic, two of which were broken beyond repair and one that had no barrel at all. Undeterred by their meager arsenal, they set off into the stormy night, determined to bring back some meat for dinner.

As they walked, the wind howling around them like a chorus of restless spirits, they finally stumbled upon a lush meadow where a majestic hare was grazing. The brothers took aim and fired, but alas, their shots missed the mark by a wide margin.

"What shall we do now?" asked one of the brothers, his voice shaking with frustration.

Just then, he remembered that their wise and benevolent godmother lived in a nearby village. They knocked on her door, and to their surprise, she answered from within, "Ah, my dear children! What brings you to my doorstep?"

The brothers explained their situation, and the godmother offered them three ancient pots from her kitchen, two of which were cracked beyond repair and one that had no bottom at all. The brothers chose the pot with no bottom and placed the hare inside it to cook.

As they waited for the hare to finish cooking, one of the brothers suggested that they ask their godmother if she had any fruit trees in her garden. She replied, "Ah, yes! I have three walnut trees, two of which are dead and one that has never borne any nuts. Feel free to pick as many as you like!"

The brother who went to shake the barren tree was careless and knocked off a nut that fell onto his hat, breaking his heel in the process. The brothers picked up some nuts and returned to their godmother's house, where they found the hare cooked to perfection.

As they pondered what to do with their bounty, one of them suggested that they take it to the nearby village, where many people were suffering from illness. They posted a notice on the street, offering free broth to anyone who wanted some. The villagers flocked to get their share, and the brothers took it away in a salad basket, using a skimmer to serve it out.

However, one outsider who was not from the village drank so much of the broth that he began to weaken. The brothers realized they needed to find a way to cure him quickly.

They sought out three physicians, each with their own unique affliction: one was blind, one deaf, and one dumb. The blind physician examined the man's tongue by feeling it with his fingers, while the deaf physician asked how he was feeling in a loud voice that no one could hear. The dumb physician wrote down some instructions on paper.

The physicians told them to go to the apothecary and buy two cents' worth of a mysterious medicine. They were to put it wherever they pleased, and the man would recover eventually. With that, the physicians bid farewell and left the brothers to their task.

The brothers purchased the medicine and returned to the village, where they administered it to the ailing stranger. Miraculously, he began to recover, and soon he was back on his feet, thanking the brothers for their kindness.

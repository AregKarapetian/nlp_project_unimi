In the misty mountains of old, there lived a humble farmer and his wife who had given birth to a precious child. They sought a wise and virtuous godparent to bless their little one with the sacred rites of passage. The father cradled the infant in his arms and ventured into the village to find this noble soul.

As he strolled along the winding path, he encountered a venerable sage, who was none other than the revered Master Lao. "I seek a just and compassionate godparent for my child," said the farmer. The Master replied, "Ah, but I am not one to judge myself worthy of such an honor." The farmer bowed his head in respect and continued on his journey.

Next, he met a gentle woman with a kind smile, who was none other than the revered Lady Meng Jiangnu. "I have been searching for a just and virtuous godparent for my child," said the farmer. The Lady replied, "Alas, I am not one to claim such a title. But go on, you will find someone who embodies justice and compassion." The farmer thanked her and pressed onward.

As he walked further, he encountered a mysterious woman with a somber aura, who was none other than the enigmatic Jiangshi. "I have been told that you are just and fair," said the farmer. Jiangshi nodded solemnly and said, "Indeed, I am just! Let us perform the sacred rites of passage for your child, and then I will show you my true nature." The farmer was taken aback but agreed to proceed.

After the baptism, Jiangshi led the farmer into a grand hall filled with lanterns that illuminated the darkness. "These are the lights of all souls in the world," said Jiangshi. "Would you like to see your own light?" The farmer's eyes widened as he beheld his own lantern, and then his son's. He saw that his own light was dwindling.

"Oh, for the love of ancestors!" cried the farmer, "may I take a little oil from my son's lamp to sustain mine?" Jiangshi shook her head firmly. "I do not grant such favors. You sought a just person, and one you have found. Now go home and prepare yourself, for I am waiting for you."

In days of yore, when the sun beat down upon the land, a young herdsman tended to his flock of mighty buffaloes. As he watched them graze, his eyes would often wander to a nearby ravine, where a she-dog would make its way at precisely noon every day. The herdsman's curiosity was piqued, and he wondered who this dog belonged to and what it did in the ravine.

One day, as the dog approached the water's edge, the herdsman hid himself behind a nearby bush. As the dog shed its skin and transformed into a beautiful maiden, the herdsman's eyes grew wide with wonder. The maiden bathed in the pools of water, her beauty shining like the sun itself. When she finished, she donned the dog skin once more and returned to the village as a canine.

The herdsman followed the maiden into the village, watching as she entered a humble abode. He inquired about the house's owner, learning that it belonged to a kind old man. The herdsman returned to his duties, but he could not shake the memory of the beautiful maiden from his mind.

As the seasons passed, the herdsman's parents began to speak of marriage, seeking a suitable bride for their son. But the young herdsman had other ideas. He announced that he would never marry a human girl, and instead wished to wed a dog. His family and friends laughed at him, thinking him mad, but he stood firm in his decision.

In the end, they relented, and the herdsman's parents asked him to name a suitable bride. He chose the maiden who transformed into a she-dog, and the old man whose house she frequented was more than happy to accept a bride price for her.

The wedding took place with great fanfare, and the herdsman's party made their way to the bride's house. The ceremony was performed with all due haste, and the newlywed couple returned to the herdsman's home. Every night, as his husband slept, the maiden would slip out of the dog skin and roam free.

But one fateful evening, the herdsman lay in wait, watching as his wife prepared to leave. He sprang from bed, grasping her and snatching the dog skin from her grasp. The skin was tossed into the fire, where it burned to ashes. From that day on, the maiden remained a woman, but one of surpassing beauty.

As news of the herdsman's good fortune spread throughout the village, people came from far and wide to congratulate him on his wisdom in marrying a dog. Among them was his friend Jitu, who had always been envious of the herdsman's luck. Jitu thought that he too could find a suitable canine bride, and so he set about wooing one.

His friends and family were more than happy to see Jitu marry, and they chose a beautiful she-dog for him. But when it came time to anoint the bride with vermilion on her forehead, she let out a fierce growl. Despite this warning sign, Jitu's party dragged her to his home, where they forcibly applied oil and turmeric.

But as soon as they turned their backs, the dog broke free and gave chase, nipping at Jitu's heels. The villagers laughed and jeered at him, calling out for him to catch his bride. But she was too quick, and Jitu was left with nothing but shame and humiliation.

Days passed, and Jitu became increasingly withdrawn, unable to bear the ridicule of his friends and family. In the end, he could take no more, and he took his own life in a fit of despair. The villagers whispered among themselves about the tragic fate of the young man who had sought to follow in the herdsman's footsteps, but ultimately failed.

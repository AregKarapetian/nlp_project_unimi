In days of yore, there lived a young herdsman who tended to his flock of buffalo. As he watched them graze beneath the scorching sun, he chanced upon a curious sight. Every noon, without fail, a she-dog would make its way to a nearby ravine, where pools of water lay hidden from prying eyes. The young herdsman's curiosity was piqued, and he wondered who this mysterious creature might be and what secrets it kept within the ravine.

Determined to uncover the truth, the young herdsman decided to keep watch. One day, when the she-dog arrived at the ravine, he hid himself behind a nearby bush and observed with great care. As the dog approached the water's edge, it shed its furry coat, revealing a beautiful maiden beneath. The maiden bathed in the pools, her beauty shining like the sun itself. When she finished, she donned the dog skin once more, transforming back into the creature that had first caught the young herdsman's eye.

Intrigued by this enchanting sight, the young herdsman followed the she-dog as it made its way to a nearby village. He watched with great interest as the dog entered a particular house and disappeared from view. When he inquired about the house, he learned that it belonged to a humble family.

The young herdsman's parents soon decided that it was time for him to take a wife, but their son had other plans. "I shall marry a dog," he declared, much to the amusement of his family and friends. They laughed at his eccentric notion, but the young herdsman remained resolute in his decision.

His parents, though perplexed by their son's choice, eventually relented. They asked him if there was any particular dog he desired as his bride, and the young herdsman replied with a name that sent shockwaves through the village: the very same she-dog he had watched at the ravine.

The master of the house laughed heartily at the notion of marrying off his daughter to a human, but eventually agreed to accept a dowry in exchange for her hand. The wedding preparations commenced, and soon the young herdsman's party arrived at the bride's house to escort their newlywed wife back to their village.

Every night, as the moon hung low in the sky, the young herdsman's wife would slip out of the dog skin and disappear into the darkness. But one fateful evening, the young herdsman lay in wait, pretending to sleep while his wife attempted to leave. Seizing the opportunity, he caught hold of her and seized the dog skin, casting it into the flames where it was reduced to ashes.

The maiden stood before him, radiant with a beauty that surpassed mortal bounds. News of their union spread quickly through the village, and soon everyone was praising the young herdsman's wisdom in marrying a creature so divine.

A friend of the young herdsman, named Jitu, heard tales of his companion's good fortune and became enamored with the idea of marrying a dog himself. His family and friends offered little resistance as he selected a bride from among their own kin. But when they attempted to anoint her forehead with vermilion, she let out a fierce growl, and despite their efforts, she broke free and ran after them.

The villagers shouted for Jitu to chase after his bride, but she refused to be caught. She bit at him with savage ferocity until he was forced to abandon the pursuit. The ridicule that followed was too much for Jitu to bear; he hung himself in shame just a few days later, his reputation forever tarnished by his ill-fated marriage.

The young herdsman's union, on the other hand, remained strong and blessed. His wife's beauty continued to shine like a beacon, illuminating their village with its radiance. And so, the young couple lived happily ever after, surrounded by the admiration of their community.

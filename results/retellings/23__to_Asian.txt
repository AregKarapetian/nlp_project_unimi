In the sweltering heat of summer's eve, Sennuccio and his wife Bedovina sat idly by the entrance of their humble abode, reluctant to retire for the night. As the stars began to twinkle in the sky, Sennuccio bade his wife shut the door, but she demurred, insisting that he should take care of it himself. Their petty squabble continued unabated, each one unwilling to yield, until finally, Sennuccio proposed a wager: whoever spoke first would be responsible for shutting the door.

Bedovina, known for her stubborn nature and aversion to exertion, agreed to this proposition, but neither she nor her husband dared utter a word, lest they incur the penalty of having to shut the door. As the night wore on, Bedovina's eyelids grew heavy with sleep, and she rose from her seat, shedding her garments as she made her way to their bedchamber.

Not long after, a young serving-man, Chonguita by name, chanced upon Sennuccio's house while returning from his master's estate. As he walked down the street, his lantern's candle flickered out, and he sought refuge in the open doorway of Sennuccio's home. "Is anyone within?" he called out, but his voice was met with silence.

Chonguita ventured further into the house, where he spotted Sennuccio sitting on a bench, his eyes wide awake but his mouth sealed shut. The young man approached him cautiously and asked for a light to relight his lantern, but Sennuccio remained mute, fearful of being forced to shut the door.

Undeterred, Chonguita continued on his way, noticing a faint glow emanating from the inner room. He entered the bedchamber, where he found Bedovina lying in bed, her face serene but her words as scarce as autumn leaves. The young man's eyes sparkled with amusement at her miserly nature, and he decided to pay her a visit.

As Chonguita lay beside Bedovina, his hands moved deftly, accomplishing their task without much fuss. Meanwhile, Sennuccio sat on the bench, watching the scene unfold before him, but saying not a word. When Chonguita departed, Bedovina arose from bed and made her way to the entrance of their home.

There, she found her husband still seated on the bench, his eyes fixed upon her with a mixture of guilt and amusement. "A fine husband you are!" she scolded him. "You have left me vulnerable to any unwanted visitor, and yet you failed to lift a finger to protect our home." Sennuccio rose from his seat, a sly grin spreading across his face.

"Now it is your turn, my dear," he said, his voice dripping with satisfaction. "You thought you had outwitted me, but in the end, you are the one who must shut the door. You see, headstrong folk like yourself often suffer the consequences of their own making." Bedovina's face fell as she realized her loss, but a hint of mirth still lingered in her eyes.

With a resigned sigh, she rose to shut the door, and then returned to bed beside her husband, who smiled knowingly at her. The night had been filled with unexpected twists and turns, but in the end, Sennuccio's cunning had prevailed.

In days of yore, a wily crocodile had made its lair in the embankment of a village tank. But alas! The mud had dried and hardened, trapping the beast within. Its fate seemed sealed, for it could not escape.

As fortune would have it, a traveler chanced upon the scene on his way to fetch a midwife to attend to his wife's confinement. Hearing the crocodile's plaintive cries, he inquired as to its distress. The reptile explained its predicament and begged the man to break up the earth so that it might be freed.

Moved by compassion, the traveler obliged, loosening the hardened mud with a mighty blow of his axe. The crocodile emerged from its prison, grateful for the stranger's kindness. However, as they stood on the riverbank, the crocodile seized the man's arm and prepared to make a meal of him.

"Why do you intend to devour me?" the traveler asked, incredulous. "Have you forgotten that I saved your life just now? And yet, you would repay my good deed with cruelty?"

The crocodile shrugged its scaly shoulders. "It is true, I was in your debt, but hunger has taken hold of me. I must eat to survive."

The man thought for a moment before responding, "Very well, if fate decrees that I shall be your meal, then let us seek the counsel of witnesses to this transaction." And so they set off to gather testimony from various creatures.

Their first stop was beneath a venerable kumbuk tree. The traveler posed his question: "O noble tree, we have come to ask for your opinion on this matter. This crocodile, whom I saved from death, now intends to eat me. Is such behavior just?"

The ancient tree's branches rustled in the breeze as it replied, "Alas, O crocodile, do not let that man go! He is a wicked soul who dwells beneath my shade, yet he steals my bark and leaves, eventually cutting down my very trunk for his own gain."

Undeterred by this judgment, the traveler continued on to seek the counsel of a wise cow. "O gentle cow," he asked, "I saved this crocodile from perishing. Now it seeks to devour me. Do you think such behavior is just?"

The cow's lowing echoed through the air as she spoke: "O crocodile, do not let that man go! He is a cruel and heartless creature who takes our milk for his own sustenance and ultimately kills us for his pleasure."

Finally, they came to a cunning jackal, who listened intently to their tale. The traveler posed the question once more: "O wise jackal, I saved this crocodile from death, but now it intends to eat me. What say you to this matter?"

The jackal's bushy tail twitched with curiosity as he asked, "Tell me the whole story from the beginning, and then I shall render my verdict."

Thus, the man led the crocodile back to its burrow, where it was once again imprisoned beneath a pile of earth. The jackal, now convinced that the traveler's tale was true, declared: "Do not be afraid, O traveler! I am on your side. Beat the crocodile until it is dead, for I have witnessed its wickedness and your own kindness."

With these words, the traveler seized his club and dealt a mighty blow to the crocodile, ending its life. And so, justice was served, thanks to the wise counsel of the kumbuk tree, the cow, and the cunning jackal.

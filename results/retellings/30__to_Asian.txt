In a small village nestled between two great mountains, there lived a humble tailor and his wife, who was also skilled with needle and thread. They dwelled in a cozy home, where the wife took pride in keeping a tidy household. One day, their tranquil life was disrupted by the discovery of shattered dishes and broken pots.

The husband's anger flared like a wildfire as he confronted his wife. "Who broke these things?" he demanded, his voice trembling with rage. The wife, her face set in a stubborn expression, replied, "I did it with my scissors." Her words were met with skepticism by the husband, who retorted, "Scissors? How can that be?"

The husband's ire grew as he questioned his wife further, and she continued to insist that it was indeed her scissors that had caused the destruction. In a fit of rage, the husband tied a rope around her waist and began to lower her into the well at the edge of their village.

As she descended deeper into the well, the wife's obstinacy only intensified. Her husband shouted up at her, "What did you use to break these things?" But she remained resolute in her denial, repeating, "It was my scissors!" The husband's patience wore thin as he continued to lower her into the water.

Finally, with a splash, his wife disappeared beneath the surface of the well. Her husband stood at the edge, his heart heavy with sorrow and regret. He realized too late that his actions had been rash and unjust. In desperation, he called out to his wife, "Are you satisfied now? Will you finally tell me what broke these things?" But it was too late.

The wife's hand emerged from the water, her fingers moving in a swift, cutting motion as if she were still wielding scissors. The husband's eyes widened in understanding and horror. He knew that he had been deceived by his own anger. With a heavy heart, he pulled his wife out of the well, but it was too late to undo the damage.

As they stood together on dry land, the wife's silence spoke louder than any words could have. The husband realized that he had lost not only the truth about the broken dishes but also the trust and respect of his beloved wife. From that day forward, their relationship would never be the same.

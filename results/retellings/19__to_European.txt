In days of yore, in a far-off kingdom, there lived a just and benevolent monarch who had mastered the art of understanding the language of beasts. One sunny afternoon, as he strolled through his gardens, he overheard the birds singing sweet melodies to their king, praising him for providing them with sustenance and shelter.

The king's face lit up with joy at hearing their words, and he couldn't help but chuckle to himself. His queen, who was nearby, inquired about the reason behind his merriment. The king, however, remained tight-lipped, knowing that if he revealed the secret of understanding animal speech, it would be his downfall.

The queen's curiosity got the better of her, and she pressed him for an explanation. "If you don't tell me," she said, "I shall take my leave, or perhaps even take a leap into the well." The king, unable to resist his wife's importunity, finally relented. He decided that he would rather reveal the secret than see her harm herself.

As he made his way back to the gardens, the animals sensed that something was amiss. A wise old cock, who had been watching from afar, spoke up, "Your Majesty is about to meet his demise. We no longer wish to receive your benevolence, for we fear it will bring us harm." The king laughed at this, thinking it a jest.

However, the queen's suspicions were piqued once more. She asked her husband what had caused him to laugh again. This time, he fabricated a tale about planning to construct a grand tank in honor of their kingdom. The queen, pleased with his idea, suggested that they gather all the animals from Ceylon and build it together.

The king agreed, and soon the animals were brought before them. He showed them a site for the tank and instructed them to begin construction. However, as the days passed, it became apparent that the animals were merely pretending to work. A sly jackal had been observing from afar, not lifting a finger to help with the task.

The king, curious about the jackal's inactivity, approached him and asked why he wasn't contributing to the effort. The cunning creature replied, "I was simply perusing an account, Your Majesty." Intrigued, the king pressed for more information. The jackal explained that he had been calculating the ratio of males to females in their kingdom.

The king, amused by this clever response, asked the jackal to reveal his findings. The jackal declared that, based on his calculations, there were indeed more females than males in their land. However, the king was skeptical and decided to verify the claim himself.

Upon returning home, he consulted the royal records and discovered that the opposite was true: men outnumbered women. He called for the jackal once more and informed him of this new information. The cunning creature retorted, "Your Majesty's account is flawed. If you were to include all the males who are swayed by the words of their female counterparts, it would indeed tip the balance in favor of the females."

The king was taken aback by the jackal's wit and realized that he had been outsmarted. He decided to put an end to the charade once and for all. "Wild animals," he declared, "you shall return to the heart of the forest." And with that, he dismissed them from his presence.

As the queen inquired about the status of their tank-building project, the king's patience finally wore thin. He took a sturdy cane and began to chastise her for her meddling. The queen, realizing she had overstepped, apologized profusely and vowed never to pry into matters that were none of her concern.

The king, now aware of the jackal's cunning nature, couldn't help but admire his wit and cleverness. And so, the kingdom continued on its merry way, with the wise old jackal watching from afar, ever ready to offer his sharp tongue and clever insights whenever needed.

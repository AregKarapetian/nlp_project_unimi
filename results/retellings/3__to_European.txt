In days of yore, a devout monk sat on the seashore, his heart filled with compassion for all living creatures. As he gazed out to sea, a kite soared overhead, carrying a tiny mouse in its talons. The mouse escaped and fell into the monk's lap. Moved by pity, the monk gently wrapped the mouse in a leaf and brought it back to his hermitage.

Fearful that his brethren might not be inclined to care for the little creature, he prayed to the Almighty to transform it into a young maiden. And lo! The mouse was transformed into a beautiful young girl, endowed with radiant beauty and charm.

The monk presented the young woman to his wife, saying, "Behold, my dear sister, this child who belongs to me. I beseech you to treat her as our own daughter." As she grew in age and wisdom, the monk approached her and said, "My dear child, thou art now a grown woman, and it is time for thee to marry. Choose thy husband from among the mighty ones of this world, that I may unite thee with him."

The young woman replied, "Since you give me leave to choose, I desire a husband who is the most powerful in all the land." The monk's eyes widened in surprise, and he said, "Perhaps thou wouldst have the great Sun as thy husband?" He set forth to find the Sun, and when he arrived at its radiant throne, he asked if it would be willing to marry the young woman.

The Sun replied, "I am indeed mighty, but I know of one who is more powerful than I. It is the Cloud that obscures my light and dulls my warmth." The monk sought out the Cloud, which said, "And I, in turn, know of one who is mightier than I. Go find the Wind, which drives me from east to west."

The monk pursued the Wind, which declared, "I am strong, but there is one who is more powerful than I. It is the great Mountain that I cannot move." Finally, the monk came before the Mountain, which said, "I too have met my match in a creature so small and insignificant that it bores into me and makes its home within my depths."

The monk then sought out this tiny but mighty creature – a wise and cunning Rat. He asked if the Rat would be willing to marry the young woman, but the Rat replied, "How could I possibly do so? My humble abode is narrow, and a rat only marries a mouse." The monk, understanding the Rat's words, prayed to the Almighty once more.

This time, he asked that the young woman be transformed back into her original form – a tiny mouse. And lo! She resumed her former shape, and with joy in her heart, she scampered away with her beloved Rat.

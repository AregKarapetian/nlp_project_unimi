In the village of Azizi, where the air was sweet with the scent of blooming flowers, there lived a young prince named Kofi. His parents, wise and just leaders, desired to see him married to a woman worthy of his love and devotion. But Kofi was resolute: he would not settle for any bride until he had found one who could claim the title of "the most sensitive heart in all the land."

With determination etched on his face, Kofi set out on his journey, traversing villages and towns, meeting women from all walks of life. His first stop was at the home of a woman named Akua, whose head was swathed in bandages. "What troubles you, dear one?" he asked gently. Akua explained that her maid had been careless with her hair, yanking out a strand that now pained her terribly. Kofi's heart went out to her, but he thought to himself, "This is not the one I seek."

Undeterred, he continued his search, arriving at the residence of a woman named Ama, whose body was wrapped in fine linens. She appeared sorrowful, and when asked about her distress, revealed that a minor wrinkle in her bedsheet had caused her great discomfort. Kofi's thoughts were the same as before: "This is not the one I seek."

His quest led him to the home of a woman named Efia, who sat in an easy chair, her foot bound with bandages. Tears streamed down her face as she recounted how a gentle breeze had blown a jasmine petal onto her skin, causing her such anguish. Kofi's heart was moved by her tale, and he thought, "This one is truly sensitive, for she feels the smallest of hurts deeply."

Convinced that he had found his match, Kofi proposed to Efia, who accepted with joy. Together, they were married in a grand ceremony, surrounded by their friends and family.

As the storyteller's voice trailed off, the listeners were left wondering: did Kofi make a wise choice? Only time would tell, but for now, the tale of the sensitive heart remained etched in their minds like a beautiful proverb: "A gentle breeze can stir great sorrow."

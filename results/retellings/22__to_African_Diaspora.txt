In the village of Azizi, where the sun dipped into the horizon like a ripe mango, there lived a prince with a heart full of wonder. His parents, wise and kind, urged him to find a bride, but he refused, saying, "I will not marry until I have found a woman whose sensitivity is as vast as the savannah." They replied, "Then go forth and seek her out!"

The prince set off on his journey, his feet carrying him across rivers that flowed like life itself. He came to a village where women were gathered at the well, their laughter echoing through the air. Among them was a woman with a bandaged head, her eyes closed in pain. "What troubles you, sister?" he asked.

"Oh," she replied, "my maid's careless hands have pulled out one of my hairs, leaving me to suffer." The prince thought to himself, "This is not the one I seek. Her sensitivity is as fragile as a reed."

He continued on his way, passing through fields where the wind whispered secrets to the crops. He came upon another woman, swathed in fine linens, her face pale as the moon. "What ails you, dear?" he asked.

"I am ill," she said, "for last night's bedsheet had a wrinkle that has left me weak." The prince thought again, "This is not the one I seek. Her sensitivity is as delicate as a flower."

He pressed on, his heart beating like a drum in the distance. He came to a village where women sat under trees, their voices weaving a tapestry of stories. Among them was a woman with a bandaged foot, her eyes streaming tears like the rivers that nourished the land.

"What troubles you, sister?" he asked.

"A breeze," she said, "has blown a jasmine petal onto my skin, leaving me to cry out in pain." The prince thought for a moment, and then his heart spoke, "You are the one I seek. Your sensitivity is as boundless as the sky."

And so, he married her, binding their hearts together like two rivers flowing into one. But did he do right? Ah, that is a question for the ages, a mystery that only time will reveal.

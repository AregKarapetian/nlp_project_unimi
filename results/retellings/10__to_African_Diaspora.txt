In a village nestled between two great rivers, there lived a wise woman with two daughters as different as night and day. One was kind-hearted and gentle, while the other was stubborn and disobedient.

One sunny afternoon, their mother asked the bad one to fetch a bucket of water from the well. But she refused, saying she would not obey her mother's commands. The good daughter, however, volunteered to go, despite knowing it would be a difficult task.

As she descended into the well, the bucket slipped from her hands and fell to the bottom. She hesitated, thinking about the scolding she might receive if she returned empty-handed. So, with a deep breath, she climbed down into the darkness.

At the bottom of the well, she found a narrow passage with three doors. She knocked on each one, asking if they had seen her bucket and cord. The first door belonged to a wise and kind saint, who replied, "No, child, I have not seen them." The second door was answered by a sly and wicked devil, who snarled at the good girl for being so obedient.

But when she knocked on the third door, it swung open to reveal the Madonna herself. She smiled warmly and said, "Ah, my child, yes, I have your bucket and cord. But first, I need your help. My little son is waiting for his soup, and the house needs tidying. Will you stay with me while I am away?"

The good girl agreed to help, and as she swept the floor, she found not dirt but precious coral and other treasures. She set them aside, thinking to give them to the Madonna when she returned.

When the Madonna came back, she asked if her tasks had been completed. The good girl replied that they had, but pointed out the beautiful things she had found. The Madonna smiled and said, "Keep these for yourself. Would you like a dress of calico or one of silk?" The good girl chose the calico, but the Madonna gave her the silk instead.

Next, the Madonna asked if she preferred a brass thimble or a silver one. Again, the good girl chose the wrong option, and the Madonna gave her the other. Finally, she handed over the bucket and cord, saying, "When you leave this passage, look up into the air."

As the good girl emerged from the well, she gazed upwards, and a beautiful star fell onto her forehead. She returned home to find her mother scolding her for being away so long. But when the mother saw the star shining brightly on her daughter's brow, she was amazed.

"Where have you been?" she asked, "and who put that mark on your forehead?"

The good girl explained what had happened, and the Madonna's blessing became clear to all. The mother realized that she had favored the bad daughter over the good one, and this was a lesson for her to learn.

Not long after, the bad daughter decided to follow in her sister's footsteps. She too climbed down into the well, let the bucket fall, and knocked on each door. But when she reached the Madonna's door, she did not hesitate to eat the soup meant for the child and sweep up the dirt instead of finding treasures.

When the Madonna returned, she asked if the tasks had been completed. The bad daughter lied, saying they had been done perfectly. But the Madonna saw through her deceit and gave her a calico dress when she asked for silk, and a brass thimble when she wanted silver.

Finally, the Madonna handed over the bucket and cord, saying, "When you leave this passage, look up into the air." The bad daughter emerged from the well, gazed upwards, and a lump of dirt fell onto her forehead. It soiled her face, and she returned home in anger, scolding her sister for being blessed with the star.

Her mother tried to wash away the dirt, but it only grew worse. Then she understood that the Madonna had shown her the error of her ways – favoring the bad daughter over the good one.

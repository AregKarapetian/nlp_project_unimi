The tale of the cunning rabbit and his clever tricks on the sly wolf.

In a small clearing surrounded by tall trees, there lived a quick-witted rabbit named Kaito. He had always been a thorn in the side of the wicked wolf, Ryūjin, who delighted in causing trouble for Kaito's family. Every time Kaito built a new home, Ryūjin would swoop in and destroy it, taking one of Kaito's children with him.

Kaito grew tired of this game and decided to take matters into his own hands. He gathered skilled craftsmen and together they built a sturdy plank house on a solid rock foundation. The house was designed with safety in mind, complete with a hidden cellar where the little rabbits could seek refuge during times of turmoil.

Ryūjin, however, was not one to give up easily. One day, as Kaito was out visiting his neighbor, Mrs. Tanuki, he heard a commotion outside. He rushed back home to find Ryūjin standing at the door, covered in mud and panting heavily. "Oh, please save me, Kaito!" Ryūjin cried. "The dogs are after me! Hide me somewhere they won't find me!"

Kaito, feigning kindness, ushered Ryūjin into a nearby chest. Down came the lid, and with a satisfying thud, it shut tight. Kaito then busied himself making tea, boring holes in the chest for air, and adding more wood to the fire to keep Ryūjin warm.

As the little rabbits peeked out from their hiding place, they couldn't help but stifle their giggles at the absurd situation. Meanwhile, Kaito continued to tend to Ryūjin's needs, all while secretly plotting his next move.

Finally, with a sly grin spreading across his face, Kaito poured hot water onto the chest lid. The scalding liquid seeped through the holes he had bored earlier, and Ryūjin let out a pained yelp as the water soaked through to his skin. "The fleas are biting me!" he cried, but it was too late.

With one final glance at the suffering wolf, Kaito called in his neighbors to celebrate their victory. From that day on, Ryūjin's hide hung proudly on the back porch of Kaito's house, a reminder of the cunning rabbit's cleverness and the wicked wolf's downfall.

As the sun set over the clearing, Kaito sat by the fire, puffing on his pipe and chuckling to himself. He had outwitted Ryūjin once again, and his family could finally live in peace. The little rabbits played and laughed together, their bellies full of laughter and joy. And Kaito, the clever rabbit, smiled knowingly, for he knew that with cunning and wit, even the most wicked of foes could be defeated.

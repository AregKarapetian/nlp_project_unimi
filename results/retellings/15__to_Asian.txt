In a bamboo forest, a humble woodsman was gathering firewood when he stumbled upon a serpent crushed beneath a massive stone. He carefully lifted the stone with his axe handle, freeing the snake from its predicament. As the serpent slithered away, it hissed at the woodsman, "I will devour you for your kindness." The woodsman replied, "Patience, my friend. Let us first seek the counsel of a wise one to determine if I am worthy of punishment."

Their search led them to a withered nag tied to a ancient oak tree. Its once-luxuriant coat now hung in tatters, and its eyes shone with desperation. The snake asked the nag, "Is it just for me to devour this man who saved my life?" The nag's voice trembled as he replied, "Indeed, it is only right. I was once a proud steed, carrying my master through countless battles. Now, I am nothing but a shadow of my former self, tied to this tree and fed scraps. Eat the woodsman, for he who does good shall suffer while he who does evil shall prosper."

As they continued their journey, they came across a mulberry tree, its branches gnarled with age and its leaves long gone. The snake asked the ancient tree if it was just to devour the woodsman, who had saved its life. The tree's voice creaked with age as it replied, "Yes, it is only right. I have given my master countless mulberries, which he has used to raise the finest silkworms in the land. Now that I am no longer productive, he plans to discard me like a worn-out tool. Eat the woodsman, for you will be doing a noble deed."

Their search led them to a cunning fox, who listened intently as the woodsman pleaded his case. The fox said, "I must see the situation for myself before rendering judgment." They returned to the spot where the snake was crushed beneath the stone. But when they arrived, the woodsman merely pointed at the serpent and said, "You will remain there, under that stone."

The fox, in payment for her services, requested a bag of chickens from the woodsman. He promised to deliver them the next morning. The following day, the fox arrived, but the woodsman had tricked her by filling the bag with dogs instead of chickens. As she carried the bag away, the dogs burst forth and devoured the fox. And so it is in this world: those who do good are often ill rewarded, while those who do evil prosper.

The moral of the story spread throughout the land like a whispered secret, reminding all who heard it that kindness and goodness may not always be repaid as they should be.

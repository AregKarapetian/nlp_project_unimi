In days of yore, a humble farmer and his wife, weary from their labors, sat by the fire after supper. A gust of wind had blown open the door, causing them to bicker over who should close it. "Wife, see to the door!" said the man. "Husband, 'tis your duty," retorted the woman. The husband then proposed a wager: let the one who spoke first be responsible for shutting the door. His wife, pleased with this arrangement, agreed, and they retired to bed in silence.

Later that night, as they lay abed, they heard a strange noise. Peering out into the darkness, they beheld a wild beast, its eyes glowing in the dark, rummaging through their store of provisions. Yet neither spoke a word, lest they break the agreement. The creature, sated with food, departed as suddenly as it had appeared.

The next morning, while his wife was away at the mill, the barber chanced upon the farmer and asked how he came to be alone in the house. The man remained mute, not uttering a syllable. The barber, perplexed by this silence, attempted to rouse him with a shave, but still no words escaped the farmer's lips. Undeterred, the barber continued his ministrations, shaving off half the farmer's beard and mustache, then covering his face in lampblack.

Finally, driven to distraction, the barber cried out that the man was bewitched and hastily departed. Hardly had he gone when the wife returned from her errand, only to behold her husband transformed into a pitiful sight. She trembled with fear and exclaimed, "Ah, wretch! What sorcery has befallen thee?" The farmer's response was immediate: "You spoke first, woman; now begone, and shut the door!"

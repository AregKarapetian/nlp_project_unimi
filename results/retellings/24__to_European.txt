In a humble cottage nestled among the rolling hills of the countryside, an elderly couple sat by the fire after a long day's toil. The wind had blown open the door, and a heated argument ensued between them as to who should close it. "Wife, see to the door!" said the husband. "Husband, you should tend to it," retorted the wife. "Let us not speak a word until one of us utters the first," proposed the man. His wife agreed, and they retired to their chamber in silence.

In the dead of night, a fierce hound burst into the cottage, devouring the meager stores of food that lay within. Yet neither the husband nor wife dared utter a sound, lest they be forced to shut the door as per their earlier agreement. The dog, sated, departed as suddenly as it arrived.

The next morning, while the wife was away at the mill, grinding grain into flour, a barber chanced upon the silent farmer. "What sorcery is this?" exclaimed the barber, observing the man's mute state. He shaved the husband's head, then half his beard and mustache, but still the poor fellow remained speechless. Finally, the barber daubed him in lampblack from head to toe. Convinced that a wicked spell had been cast upon the farmer, he hastily departed.

Just as the barber fled, the wife returned home, her eyes widening at the sight of her husband's grotesque appearance. "Wretch!" she cried, trembling with fear. "What have you done?" The farmer gazed at her sternly and said, "You spoke the first word, woman; now begone, and shut the door yourself."

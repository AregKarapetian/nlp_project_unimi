In a small village nestled between two great rivers, there lived an elderly couple who tilled the land with their own hands. One evening, as they sat by the fire after a long day's labor, a disagreement arose over who should close the door that had swung open in the wind. "My child," said the woman, "you must shut it." But the man replied, "No, I will not do it. Let us see who speaks first."

The wife, pleased with this proposal, smiled and nodded in agreement. The couple retired to their bed, content in their silence.

But as night fell, a strange noise echoed through the room. They peeked out from under the blanket and saw that a wild dog had entered, devouring their meager store of food. Yet neither of them spoke a word, nor did they try to shoo the beast away. The dog finished its meal and left, leaving the couple to their stillness.

The next morning, while the woman was out at the mill, grinding grain into flour, the barber came to visit the man. "What's wrong, my friend?" he asked, but the farmer remained mute. The barber shaved his head, then half of his beard and mustache, but still the man said nothing.

The barber, perplexed, covered the farmer in a thick layer of lampblack. But even this did not stir him to speak. "This man is cursed!" cried the barber, and he hastily departed.

Just as the barber was leaving, the woman returned from the mill, her face pale with worry. She saw her husband's transformed appearance and trembled with fear. "What have you done?" she exclaimed.

The farmer smiled slyly and said, "You spoke first, my child. Now it is your turn to shut the door."

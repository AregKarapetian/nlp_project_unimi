In a small village nestled in the rolling hills of the countryside, there lived an old crone who was feared and reviled by all who knew her. They whispered among themselves that she was a sorceress, imbued with dark powers and malevolent intentions. One day, as a young maiden named Chonguita passed by the crone's humble abode, the old woman beckoned her to enter.

Chonguita, curious and innocent, stepped inside and found herself in a room filled with exquisite tapestries and paintings. The crone offered her a basket of juicy apples, which Chonguita devoured with relish. As she finished eating, the crone proposed that Chonguita remain with her as her companion.

But Chonguita's mother had entrusted her with an errand to procure some provisions for supper. She excused herself, promising to return soon. The crone, however, seemed reluctant to let her go, and with a flick of her wand, the table was filled with an assortment of delicacies from Chonguita's own home.

Chonguita agreed to stay with the crone for a week, and as the days passed, she began to experience strange and vivid dreams. In each dream, a mischievous sprite appeared to warn her that the crone intended to do her harm. The sprite left her a comb and a handkerchief, instructing her to drop them when the crone made her escape.

One fateful night, Chonguita fled through the window and dropped the comb behind her. A sturdy oak tree sprouted forth from the earth, its branches entwining the crone's path. Undeterred, she pressed on, only to be confronted by a river that flowed from the handkerchief, forcing her to wade across.

As Chonguita neared her home, she saw the crone approaching, but just as they were about to meet, a woodsman appeared and struck off the crone's head with his axe. He escorted Chonguita back to her relieved mother, who scolded her for disobeying her instructions in the first place.

In days of yore, in a village nestled between two great rivers, there lived a young lass named Käthe. The villagers whispered among themselves about an ancient crone who dwelled in a nearby cottage, a woman so feared and reviled that they dared not speak her name aloud. As Käthe passed by the cottage, the crone beckoned her inside with a gnarled finger.

The interior of the cottage was a marvel to behold: walls adorned with vibrant tapestries, shelves lined with glittering trinkets, and a fire crackling in the hearth that seemed to dance with an otherworldly light. The crone offered Käthe a basket of succulent apples, which she devoured with relish. As they ate, the crone proposed a most unusual arrangement: Käthe would remain by her side, forsaking all duties and obligations.

But Käthe's thoughts soon turned to her mother's summons, sent in haste for a vital ingredient to prepare supper. She excused herself, promising to return anon. The crone, however, pressed her to stay, offering a wand that seemed to shimmer with dark magic. With a tap of the wand upon the table, a bounty of provisions appeared at Käthe's mother's doorstep, as if conjured by unseen hands.

Käthe agreed to remain for a week, and during that time, she began to experience strange and unsettling visions. In her dreams, an elfin figure warned her of impending doom: the crone would seek to do her harm. The elf left behind two tokens: a delicate comb and a snowy handkerchief. Käthe was instructed to drop them when the crone came in pursuit.

One fateful night, as she fled through the darkness, Käthe cast aside the comb, and a mighty forest sprouted from the earth, its branches entwining the cottage like skeletal fingers. The crone's progress was slowed, but not halted. Undeterred, Käthe dropped the handkerchief next, and a river burst forth, its waters swirling around the cottage.

Finally, as the crone stumbled through the woods, a woodcutter chanced upon her, axe in hand. With a swift blow, he severed the crone's head from her shoulders. The villagers hailed Käthe's return with joy, but her mother's words of warning still echoed: "Beware the consequences of disobedience."

In a small village nestled between two great mountains, there lived a revered monk who possessed a gift from the heavens: his prayers were always answered with haste and precision. One day, as he sat on the shore of a tranquil lake, watching the sun set behind the waves, a magnificent kite soared above him, its strings dancing in the breeze. Attached to one of its claws was a tiny mouse, which had managed to escape and fallen onto the monk's lap.

Moved by compassion for the tiny creature, the monk gently picked it up and wrapped it in a large leaf, cradling it as if it were a precious jewel. He then carried it back to his humble abode, where he feared that his people might not welcome the mouse with open arms. So, he prayed to the Great Spirit above, beseeching him to transform the mouse into a young maiden of great beauty.

Instantly, the mouse was transformed, and before the monk's very eyes, it became a lovely young girl with skin as smooth as jade and hair as black as the night sky. The monk presented her to his wife, saying, "Behold! This is my child, whom I have brought home to be cared for by you." His wife welcomed the young girl warmly, treating her like one of their own.

As the years passed, the young girl grew into a beautiful woman, with skin as radiant as the full moon and hair that cascaded down her back like a waterfall. The monk approached her one day, saying, "My dear daughter, you have reached the age where marriage is fitting. Choose for yourself a husband who suits your tastes, and I shall arrange for you to be united with him."

The young girl thought deeply before responding, "I desire a husband who holds the greatest power in all the world." The monk's eyes widened in surprise, but he replied, "Perhaps you would like to marry the great Dragon King himself?" He set off towards the eastern mountains, where the mighty dragon resided.

However, upon arriving at the dragon's lair, the monk was told by the wise beast that it was not the most powerful being in all the world. Instead, it pointed out a majestic cloud that drifted lazily across the sky, its soft peaks hiding the sun from view. The monk sought out the cloud and repeated the young girl's request.

The cloud replied, "I am not the most powerful, for I am constantly at the mercy of the Wind God." So, the monk continued his journey to find the wind, which was said to roam freely across the land, shaping mountains and valleys with its mighty breath. The wind, however, claimed that it too was not the most powerful, pointing out a towering mountain range in the distance.

The monk climbed the highest peak, where he met an ancient sage who revealed to him that the true master of power resided within the earth itself – a cunning rat who burrowed deep into the mountainside. The rat, with its razor-sharp teeth and agile claws, was said to be able to tunnel through solid stone.

Finally, the monk arrived at the rat's underground lair and asked if it would consent to marry the young girl. But the rat laughed, saying, "How could I possibly marry a mouse? My home is narrow, and only a fellow rodent can share my humble abode." The monk realized that his daughter had fallen in love with the very creature she was meant to wed.

Moved by their devotion, the monk prayed once more to the Great Spirit above. This time, he asked for the young girl to be transformed back into her original form – a tiny mouse. Instantly, she resumed her former shape and scurried off with the rat, who welcomed her as his mate in the underground tunnels of their mountain home.

From that day on, the monk would often look up at the stars and smile, knowing that his prayers had brought joy to two creatures who loved each other deeply.

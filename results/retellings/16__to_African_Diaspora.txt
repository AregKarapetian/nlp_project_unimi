In the village of Kanduru, where the sun dipped into the horizon like a ripe mango, there lived a man named Thambi. He was on his way to fetch a midwife for his wife, who was in labor with their first child. As he walked along the embankment of the tank, he heard a faint cry for help. A crocodile, stuck in its burrow, begged him to break up the earth so it could escape.

Thambi, being a just man, obliged the crocodile's request and freed it from its prison. But as they made their way to the river, Thambi noticed that the tank was dry. He placed the crocodile on his shoulder and carried it to the edge of the water. The crocodile, still hungry, seized Thambi by the arm and prepared to devour him.

"Why do you want to eat me?" Thambi asked, puzzled. "Did I not help you when you were in trouble? And now you repay me with death?"

The crocodile replied, "You are right, I was grateful for your kindness. But hunger is a mighty force, and it drives even the wisest of creatures to do foolish things."

Thambi thought for a moment and said, "It is good that you will eat me, but first let us ask some witnesses about this matter." He led the crocodile to a kumbuk tree, which stood tall and proud like a guardian of the forest.

"Respected kumbuk tree," Thambi said, "this crocodile was going to die, but I saved it. Now it is going to eat me. Is that right?"

The kumbuk tree spoke in its deep, rumbling voice, "O crocodile, do not let that man go. He is a wicked creature, always taking what does not belong to him. He stays under my shade, breaks off my bark and leaves, and eventually cuts down the whole tree."

Thambi nodded his head in understanding and continued on his journey with the crocodile. They came across a cow, who was grazing peacefully by the river.

"Respected cow," Thambi said, "I saved this crocodile from death. Now it is going to eat me. Do you think that is right?"

The cow replied, "O crocodile, do not let that man go. He takes our milk and eventually kills us for his own pleasure. Do not let him live."

Thambi thanked the cow and continued on his way with the crocodile. They finally came across a wise old jackal, who was sitting by the river, watching the world go by.

"What is it all about?" the jackal asked, its eyes twinkling with curiosity.

Thambi explained the whole story to the jackal, from the beginning to the end. The jackal listened attentively and then said, "I must hear both sides of the story before I can give my opinion."

So Thambi placed the crocodile on his shoulder once again and carried it back to its burrow. He packed the soil in around it, making sure it was comfortable.

The jackal looked at Thambi with a stern expression and said, "Don't be afraid, I am on your side. You are a just man, and this crocodile is wicked. Take up a cudgel and beat him until he is dead."

Thambi thanked the jackal for its wisdom and did as it suggested. The crocodile was punished for its wickedness, and Thambi returned home to his wife, who had given birth to a beautiful baby boy.

As they sat by the fire, watching their child sleep, Thambi's wife asked him about his encounter with the crocodile. He told her the whole story, and she smiled knowingly.

"Ah," she said, "the world is full of wicked creatures, but we must always stand up for what is right."

In the village of Azizi, where the sun dips into the horizon like a golden egg yolk, there lived a man named Kofi. He was on his way to fetch a midwife for his wife, who was in labor with their firstborn child. As he walked along the embankment of a tank, he heard a faint cry for help. It was a crocodile, stuck in its burrow due to the dried mud.

Kofi's heart went out to the creature, and he said, "My brother, I will not leave you to die like a bird without wings." He broke up the earth with his bare hands, and the crocodile emerged from its prison. But as they made their way to the river, Kofi realized that the tank was dry, and the crocodile would perish if he didn't find water.

With the crocodile on his shoulder, Kofi walked for hours until they reached the edge of the mighty River Niger. He gently placed the crocodile into the water, but to his surprise, the creature seized him by the arm and prepared to devour him. "Why do you want to eat me?" Kofi asked, his voice trembling with fear.

The crocodile replied, "I am hungry, my friend. But I must admit that you saved my life. You are a kind soul." Kofi said, "It is good that you acknowledge my kindness. However, before you eat me, let us ask the witnesses of our village to give their opinion on this matter."

They walked for miles until they reached the shade of a majestic kumbuk tree. Kofi asked the tree, "O wise one, what do you think about this situation? Should I be eaten by this crocodile?" The tree replied, "Kofi, my child, that man is wicked. He takes our shade without giving us anything in return. He breaks off our branches and leaves, and eventually cuts down our entire being."

Next, they visited a cow who was grazing in the nearby field. Kofi asked her, "O gentle one, do you think it's right for this crocodile to eat me after I saved his life?" The cow said, "Kofi, my friend, that man is a thief. He takes our milk and eventually kills us. Do not let him go."

Finally, they came to a wise old jackal who was perched on a rock. Kofi asked him, "O wise one, what do you think about this situation? Should I be eaten by the crocodile?" The jackal said, "I need to hear the whole story before I can give my opinion." So Kofi took the crocodile back to its burrow and packed the soil around it.

The jackal said, "Do not fear, Kofi. I am on your side. You have shown great kindness to this creature, but he is wicked. He has no regard for life or property. Take up a stick and beat him until he is dead." And with that, Kofi's fate was sealed. The crocodile met its demise, and Kofi returned home to his wife, who had given birth to a beautiful baby boy.

As they sat together under the shade of their hut, Kofi's wife asked, "My husband, what happened to the crocodile?" Kofi replied, "It is said that when you show kindness to those in need, but they turn against you, it is like trying to hold water in your hands. It will eventually slip away and leave you with nothing but sorrow."

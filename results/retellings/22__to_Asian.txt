In a small village nestled in the mountains, a prince's parents urged him to find a bride worthy of his station. But he refused to settle for any woman unless she was the most sensitive soul in all the land. His parents, exasperated, told him to go out and seek her himself.

The prince set off on his journey, traveling through lush valleys and over misty mountains. He came across a maiden with a bandaged head, tears streaming down her face. "What troubles you, dear one?" he asked. She explained that her maid had accidentally pulled out a hair while combing her locks, causing her great distress. The prince thought to himself, "This is not the one I seek."

He continued on his way, and soon found another woman swathed in fine linens, looking pale and wan. "What ails you?" he inquired. She replied that a tiny wrinkle in her bedsheet had kept her awake all night, making her feel unwell. The prince pondered this for a moment before deciding she was not the sensitive one he sought.

Undeterred, he pressed on, seeking wisdom and compassion. His travels eventually led him to a third maiden, who sat in an elegant chair with a bandaged foot. Tears streamed down her face as she recounted how a gentle breeze had blown a jasmine blossom petal onto her skin while strolling in the garden. The prince listened intently, his heart moved by her tale.

After a moment's consideration, he declared, "You are indeed the most sensitive woman I have ever met! Your heart is as delicate as a cherry blossom." And with that, he proposed to her, and she accepted. They were married in a grand ceremony, surrounded by their loved ones. The prince's parents beamed with joy, grateful that their son had found a true partner.

But some say the prince made a mistake, for his new wife's sensitivity was not always a blessing...

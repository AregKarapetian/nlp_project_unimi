In a far-off kingdom, a prince sought a bride who would be known throughout the land as the most sensitive woman in the world. His parents, eager to see their son wed, urged him to find this paragon of sensitivity. The prince set out on his quest, traveling through villages and towns, meeting various women along the way.

First, he came upon a young woman whose head was swathed in bandages. "What troubles you?" he asked with concern. She replied that her maid had accidentally pulled out one of her hairpins while combing her hair, causing her great distress. The prince thought to himself, "This is not the one I seek." He continued his journey.

Next, he encountered a woman whose entire body was wrapped in fine silk. Her face was pale and wan, and she seemed on the verge of tears. "What ails you?" asked the prince. She explained that a tiny wrinkle in her bedding had kept her awake all night, making her feel unwell. The prince thought to himself, "This is not the one I seek." He pressed on.

Finally, he came upon a woman sitting in an intricately carved chair, her foot bound with bandages. Tears streamed down her face as she recounted how a gentle breeze had blown a jasmine petal onto her skin while strolling in the garden. The prince was taken aback by her sensitivity and thought to himself, "This is indeed the one I have been searching for." He proposed marriage on the spot, and the woman accepted.

The prince and his new bride returned to the kingdom, where they were welcomed with great fanfare. As they sat together in their chambers, the prince gazed at his wife with admiration, thinking that he had found the most sensitive woman in all the land. And so, the young couple lived happily ever after, surrounded by the whispers of courtiers and advisors who wondered if the prince had made a wise choice.

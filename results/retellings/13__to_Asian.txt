In a small village nestled between two great mountains, there lived a young girl named Mei. She was known for her curiosity and love for adventure. One day, as she wandered through the forest, she stumbled upon an ancient hut belonging to a wise woman, rumored to possess mystical powers by the villagers.

The wise woman, with eyes that seemed to hold secrets of the universe, beckoned Mei to enter her abode. Inside, Mei marveled at the vibrant tapestries and intricate carvings adorning the walls. The wise woman offered her a basket of juicy, crimson apples, which Mei devoured in mere moments.

As they sat together, the wise woman proposed that Mei stay with her permanently, citing the comfort and security she could provide. But Mei's thoughts were elsewhere; she had been sent on an errand by her mother to fetch a rare ingredient for dinner. She politely declined the wise woman's offer, assuring her that she would return soon.

The wise woman, however, was not one to be easily deterred. With a flick of her wrist and a whispered incantation, a bounty of fresh vegetables and fragrant herbs appeared in Mei's mother's kitchen, leaving them all bewildered by the sudden abundance.

Mei agreed to stay with the wise woman for a week, during which time she was treated to lavish feasts and tutelage in the mystical arts. But as the days passed, Mei began to experience vivid dreams, foretelling her own demise at the hands of the wise woman. Undaunted, Mei prepared herself for the worst.

One fateful night, as the wise woman's shadow loomed behind her, Mei dropped a delicate comb onto the forest floor. Instantly, a towering bamboo stalk sprouted from the earth, blocking the wise woman's path. Enraged, she pressed onward, only to be confronted by a churning river that had appeared in an instant.

As the wise woman struggled to ford the waters, Mei discarded a silk handkerchief, summoning a torrent of rain and hailstones that further impeded her progress. Finally, as the wise woman stumbled into a nearby clearing, she was met with the wrath of a woodcutter, who had been watching from afar.

With a swift swing of his axe, the woodcutter severed the wise woman's head, freeing Mei from her clutches. As they returned to Mei's village, her mother scolded her for disobeying her instructions and putting herself in harm's way.

In a small village nestled in the mountains, there lived an elderly woman named Obaachan, who was feared and avoided by the villagers for her mysterious ways. One day, a young girl named Chikako wandered into Obaachan's humble abode, where she found a beautifully decorated room filled with exquisite calligraphy and paintings. Obaachan welcomed Chikako warmly and offered her delicious rice cakes to eat.

After finishing her snack, Obaachan asked Chikako to stay with her for a while, but the girl remembered that her mother had sent her on an errand to fetch some ingredients for dinner. She politely declined Obaachan's offer, saying she would return later. However, Obaachan insisted that Chikako could have anything she desired, and with a wave of her staff, a magnificent coat appeared before the girl.

Chikako was amazed by the coat's beauty but knew it wouldn't fit her. She politely declined again, but Obaachan was insistent. She conjured up a hat to match the coat, but Chikako pointed out that it didn't suit her either. Undeterred, Obaachan created a stunning wall painting of the mountains and forests surrounding their village.

Chikako realized that Obaachan's powers were indeed extraordinary, but she still had to complete her errand for her mother. She promised to return soon, but as the days passed, Chikako began to have ominous dreams. In her visions, a wise spirit warned her of Obaachan's true intentions and instructed her to drop a comb and handkerchief when the time came.

That night, Chikako fled from Obaachan's house through the window and dropped the comb behind her. A towering bamboo grove sprouted up, blocking Obaachan's path. As she struggled to navigate the dense foliage, Chikako dropped the handkerchief, and a mighty river burst forth, forcing Obaachan to cross its waters.

Finally, as Obaachan neared Chikako's village, she stumbled upon a woodsman busy chopping down trees. The woodcutter spotted Obaachan and, with one swift swing of his axe, severed her head from her body. He escorted Chikako back home, where her mother scolded her for disobeying her instructions.

From that day forward, the villagers whispered among themselves about the mysterious events surrounding Obaachan's demise, but they never forgot the bravery and cunning displayed by young Chikako.

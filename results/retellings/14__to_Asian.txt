In the verdant village of Bamboo Grove, where cherry blossoms bloomed in abundance, Old Master Rabbit and his young son were out foraging for bamboo shoots to cook for dinner. Meanwhile, Brer Wolf had slipped into their humble abode, waiting patiently for Master Rabbit's return.

As the sun began to set, casting a warm orange glow over the village, Master Rabbit arrived home with haste. He noticed that everything seemed eerily still and silent, which made him feel uneasy. His keen eyes scanned the surroundings, taking in every detail. The front door was slightly ajar, which was unusual since his family always ensured it was securely shut.

Master Rabbit approached cautiously, his footsteps light as a spring breeze. He peered through the crack of the door but saw nothing out of the ordinary. He listened intently, his ears perked up like a bird's, but heard no signs of life within. Puzzled, he stroked his whiskers and pondered.

"Ah," he thought to himself, "the rafters know who's hiding in the attic, the bedframe knows who's sleeping beneath it, and I'm just a simple bamboo stalk – what can I do? But I must find out who's inside this house without putting myself in harm's way."

Some villagers might have rushed into the house, eager to confront whatever was lurking within. However, Master Rabbit had more sense than that. He asked only for a chance to reason with whoever was hiding behind the door.

He strolled away from the house and climbed onto a nearby bamboo stump, calling out in a clear voice, "Heyo, House! Why don't you respond?" The house stood still, its silence deafening. Brer Wolf, hiding behind the door, grew increasingly agitated, unsure of what to make of Master Rabbit's strange behavior.

Master Rabbit called out again, his voice growing louder and more insistent, "Heyo, House! Is something amiss within you?" Still, there was no response from the house. Brer Wolf began to feel a chill run down his spine as he peered through the crack in the door but saw nothing.

Undeterred, Master Rabbit continued to holler, "Heyo, House! Why have you lost your usual courtesy and not replied to me?" Brer Wolf's agitation turned to confusion. He didn't know how to react to this peculiar situation.

Finally, after several attempts, Master Rabbit waited a moment before calling out once more, "Heyo, House!" This time, Brer Wolf, trying to mimic the sound of a house responding, hollered back in a hoarse voice, "Heyo, Yo'self!"

Master Rabbit chuckled to himself and thought, "Ah-ah, Brer Wolf, that won't do. You'll have to stand out in the rain for a long time before you can talk as rough as this old house!" With a final glance at the silent house, Master Rabbit hopped off the bamboo stump and returned home, leaving Brer Wolf to slink away, defeated.

From that day on, Master Rabbit lived a peaceful life without any more troubles from the other creatures.

In the verdant heart of the bamboo forest, where the sun dipped into the horizon and painted the sky with hues of crimson and gold, Ole Miss Rabbit and little Rabs ventured out to gather sallid for dinner. Meanwhile, Brer Wolf had slipped into their humble abode, waiting patiently for Brer Rabbit's return. As the hours ticked by, Brer Rabbit grew weary from his journey, but his keen senses remained alert.

Upon approaching the house, he noticed an unsettling stillness, like a tranquil pond reflecting the beauty of nature. His eyes scanned the surroundings, and his ears perked up to detect any sign of life. The front door, usually secured with a latch, now creaked slightly on its hinges, sending a shiver down Brer Rabbit's spine.

With caution, he crept closer, his footsteps as light as a leaf rustling in the breeze. He peered through the crack between the door and the frame, but saw nothing out of the ordinary. His ears strained to pick up any sound, but the silence was oppressive, like a heavy mist that refused to lift.

Brer Rabbit pondered his next move, his mind racing with possibilities. "The pot-rack knows what's cooking in the chimney," he thought, "the rafters are aware of who's hiding in the loft, and the bed-cord is familiar with who lies beneath the bed." He chuckled to himself, "I'm no pot-rack, nor rafter, nor bed-cord, but I'll find out what's amiss without falling into the mill-pond myself."

Some might have rushed headlong into the house, only to vanish forever. But Brer Rabbit was wiser than that. He sought a more clever approach, one that would allow him to uncover the truth without putting himself in harm's way.

He retreated a short distance from the house and climbed an 'simmon stump, where he began to holler at the top of his lungs, "Heyo, House! Why not respond?" The house remained silent, its stillness unbroken. Brer Wolf, hiding behind the door, grew restless, his eyes wide with confusion.

Brer Rabbit's cries echoed through the forest, growing louder and more insistent. "Heyo, House! Heyo!" he shouted, as if trying to awaken a slumbering giant. The house stood firm, unmoving and unresponsive. Brer Wolf felt a chill run down his spine, a sensation he'd never experienced before.

Undeterred, Brer Rabbit continued to holler, his voice carrying far and wide. "Heyo, House! Ain't you got no manners left?" The forest creatures paused in their evening routines, drawn by the commotion. Brer Wolf, however, was not about to be outdone. He summoned a deep, hoarse voice from within himself, responding with a growl, "Heyo, yo'se'f!"

Brer Rabbit's eyes sparkled with amusement as he realized his opponent's attempt at mimicry. "Ha! Brer Wolf, that won't do," he exclaimed, chuckling to himself. The little rabbit leapt off the 'simmon stump, laughing so hard that he stumbled and nearly fell.

As the sun dipped below the horizon, casting the forest in a warm orange glow, Brer Rabbit's laughter echoed through the trees. And from that day forward, Brer Wolf slunk away, never to trouble the wise and cunning Brer Rabbit again. The little rabbit lived out his days in peace, free from the harassment of his cunning foe.

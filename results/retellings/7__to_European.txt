In days of yore, when the good Lord saw fit to bestow upon the world another tempestuous evening, an elderly gent, burdened by a load of firewood, found himself beset by the fury of the skies. As the storm raged on, he sought refuge in a hollow tree, where he remained until the tempest had passed.

As the darkness began to lift, and the old man prepared to resume his journey, a merry tune caught his ear, and he peeped out from his sanctuary to behold a strange sight. A coterie of demons, their faces aglow with mirth, were dancing and singing in the moonlight. Their antics, though unorthodox, proved too great a temptation for the old man's reserve, and he couldn't help but burst into laughter.

As he stood there, watching the spectacle unfold, his gaze chanced upon one demon who danced alone, his movements clumsy and awkward. The leader of the demons, a towering figure with eyes that glowed like embers, cast a disapproving glance in the fellow's direction. "Enough!" he cried. "Is there none among you who can dance with greater finesse?"

The old man, whose youth seemed to have been stirred by the demon's words, felt an inexplicable urge to reveal his own dancing prowess. In days of yore, he had been a skilled dancer, and the memory of those halcyon days now flooded back to him. He stepped forward, and with a flourish, began to dance before the demons.

Their applause was thunderous, and they begged him to perform several more dances. The old man's heart swelled with pride as they praised his skill, and when the leader asked him to return the following eve, he agreed without hesitation.

However, the demon king had one condition: the old man must leave a pledge behind him. His gaze fell upon the wen that marred the old man's right cheek, and with a wave of his hand, it vanished into thin air. The demons vanished as well, leaving the old man to make his way home, his cheek smooth and unblemished.

As he walked, he couldn't help but feel a sense of wonder at the strange turn of events. Upon arriving at his humble abode, he shared the tale with his wife, who listened with a mixture of awe and trepidation.

Their neighbor, a cantankerous old man, had long suffered from a wen on his left cheek, despite the many remedies that had been applied to it. When he heard of the old man's good fortune, he called upon him, eager to learn more about the demons and their strange powers.

The old man, still basking in the glow of his encounter with the demons, told his neighbor where he might find the hollow tree, and advised him to hide within its branches just before sunset. The wicked old man set out, determined to deceive the demons and claim their power for himself.

As night fell, he crept into the hollow tree, and soon found himself face to face with the demons once more. One of them remarked on the old man's tardiness, and the wicked fellow, emboldened by his own cunning, emerged from his hiding place, flourishing a fan in an attempt to dance.

Alas, his movements were clumsy and awkward, and the demons expressed their displeasure. "You dance with all the finesse of a drunken ox!" one of them cried. As they spoke, the demon king flung the wen at the old man's right cheek, where it remained firmly fixed.

The wicked old man, who had sought to deceive the demons, slunk away, his face marred by two wens, one on either side. And so, he learned a bitter lesson about the dangers of deception and the power of the supernatural.

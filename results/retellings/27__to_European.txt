In days of yore, when the sun shone bright and the forest creatures roamed free, there lived a wily rabbit named Herr Hase. He dwelled in a cozy little burrow, surrounded by his family, but alas! His peace was frequently disrupted by the depredations of a cunning wolf named Herr Wolf.

Herr Hase's home was repeatedly raided, and with each attack, one of his young ones fell prey to the marauder's cruelty. The rabbit's straw hut was torn asunder, then his pine-top abode, followed by his bark house. Each time, he lost a child, and Herr Hase's patience wore thin.

One day, in a fit of rage, he stormed off, vowing to outwit the wolf once and for all. He sought out skilled craftsmen, who built him a sturdy plank house with a solid rock foundation. The rabbit's new home stood firm against any assault, and Herr Hase was finally able to enjoy some respite from his troubles.

He fashioned a hiding place in the cellar, where his little ones could seek refuge when danger lurked nearby. The front door caught on the inside, ensuring that no unwanted visitor could enter uninvited. Herr Wolf, observing the rabbit's new stronghold, laid low for a while, but eventually, he grew bolder.

One afternoon, as Herr Hase was preparing to visit his neighbor, Frau Fuchs, he heard a commotion outside. Before he could even turn around, Herr Wolf burst into the house, mud-caked and panting. "Oh, please, Herr Hase! Save me!" he cried, "The dogs are after me, and they'll tear me apart! Hide me somewhere safe!"

Herr Hase, feigning concern, directed the wolf to jump into a nearby chest. Down came the lid, and the hasp caught with a satisfying click. The rabbit then strolled over to the mirror, winked at his reflection, and settled into his favorite rocking chair by the fire.

As he puffed on his pipe, Herr Hase began to ponder the wolf's predicament. "Ah, yes," he said to himself, "I must do something for my dear friend." He fetched a kettle and filled it with water, placing it over the flames. "What am I doing now, Herr Wolf?" he asked, feigning innocence.

"I'm making you a nice cup of tea!" replied Herr Hase, as he began to bore holes in the chest lid with his gimlet. The wolf's eyes grew wide with alarm, but the rabbit continued to chat and chuckle, all the while secretly plotting the wolf's demise.

As the minutes ticked by, Herr Hase added more wood to the fire, ostensibly to keep the wolf warm. He then descended into the cellar, fetched his children, and brought them back upstairs. "What am I doing now, Herr Wolf?" he asked once more.

"I'm telling my children what a kind soul you are!" The young rabbits struggled to contain their giggles as they gazed at the hapless wolf.

Finally, Herr Hase poured scalding water onto the chest lid, and the wolf's cries were drowned out by the sound of his own skin sizzling. "What's that I feel now, Herr Wolf?" asked the rabbit, feigning concern.

"The fleas are eating me alive!" The wolf's final words were spoken in agony as he succumbed to the scalding water.

Herr Hase then summoned his neighbors, and they celebrated long into the night. If you were to visit Herr Hase's home today, you might catch a glimpse of Herr Wolf's pelt hanging on the back porch, a testament to the rabbit's cunning and the wolf's ultimate downfall.

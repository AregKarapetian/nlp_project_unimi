In days of yore, when the sun shone bright and the forest creatures roamed free, there lived a wily rabbit named Chonguita. He dwelled in a cozy little burrow with his family, but alas! Br'er Wolf, that cunning canine, was always on the prowl, seeking to pilfer one of Chonguita's precious young'uns.

Chonguita, wise and resourceful as he was, built himself a straw hut, thinking it would be safe from Br'er Wolf's prying eyes. But no! The sly wolf tore down the straw hut with ease, and Chonguita lost one of his little ones in the process.

Undeterred, Chonguita constructed another dwelling, this time out of pine tops. Alas! Br'er Wolf razed it to the ground as well, claiming another young rabbit for his own.

Chonguita's patience wore thin, and he grew angry at the wolf's constant thievery. He ranted and raved, but then, with a cunning glint in his eye, he set out to build a new home – one that would withstand even Br'er Wolf's most dastardly attempts.

Chonguita enlisted the aid of skilled carpenters, who constructed for him a sturdy plank house with a rock foundation. The little rabbits could finally live in peace and quiet, while Chonguita himself could stroll about, enjoying the company of his neighbors without fear of Br'er Wolf's raids.

But one day, as Chonguita was preparing to pay a visit to the lovely Mrs. Coon, he heard the unmistakable sound of Br'er Wolf galloping up the road. The wolf burst into the house, mud-caked and panting, begging for Chonguita's mercy: "Oh, dear Chonguita! Save me from those ravenous dogs that pursue me!"

Chonguita, with a wry smile playing on his lips, directed Br'er Wolf to jump into a nearby chest. Down went the lid, and in went the hook, securing the wolf inside.

As Chonguita busied himself making tea for the "distraught" wolf, he began to bore holes in the chest lid with a gimlet. "I'm just giving you some air, Br'er Wolf," he said with a chuckle.

Next, he added more wood to the fire, so the wolf wouldn't grow cold. And when the little rabbits asked what Chonguita was doing, he replied, "I'm telling my children what a kind soul you are, Br'er Wolf."

But the pièce de résistance came when Chonguita poured scalding water onto the chest lid, causing the wolf to shriek in agony. The fleas that had been biting him began to emerge from their hiding places, and the poor wolf's cries turned to wails of despair.

As the water seeped through the holes, Br'er Wolf let out a final, pitiful cry before succumbing to his scalding fate. Chonguita, triumphant, summoned his neighbors to celebrate the wolf's demise, and from that day on, Br'er Wolf's pelt hung proudly in the back porch of Chonguita's house.

And so, dear listener, if you ever find yourself passing by Chonguita's abode, take a glance at the wall picture – it's none other than Br'er Wolf himself, looking rather forlorn and bedraggled. Ah, but that's what happens when one meddles in the affairs of others!

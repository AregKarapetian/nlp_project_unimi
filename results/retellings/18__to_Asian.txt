In a small village nestled between two great mountains, there lived a humble farmer named Kaito. He toiled from dawn till dusk, tending to his fields and oxen with dedication and care. However, his life was disrupted by the arrival of a fierce lion, who demanded one of his prized oxen every evening under threat of violence.

Kaito, fearing for his life and livelihood, gave in to the lion's demands each time, purchasing another ox to replace the one taken. The villagers whispered among themselves about the strange occurrences, but Kaito kept his troubles hidden.

One day, as Kaito was driving home with a single ox, he encountered a cunning jackal named Akira. Akira asked Kaito why he seemed to have two oxen in the morning and only one at night. Kaito confided in Akira about the lion's demands, and Akira offered to help him in exchange for a promise: if he freed Kaito from the lion's grasp, Kaito would reward him with a fine sheep.

Akira devised a plan to rid Kaito of the lion. He instructed Kaito to pretend that an Asko (a sacred block of wood) was speaking to the lion when it arrived at dusk. Akira would call out from a nearby hill, disguising his voice as the Asko's. Kaito was to respond with a loud "It is only an Asko!" and then strike the block with a hatchet.

The next day, the lion appeared as expected, demanding one of Kaito's oxen. Akira called out from the hill, asking who spoke with Kaito. The lion, trembling with fear, replied that it was the voice of the gods. Kaito, following Akira's instructions, loudly declared the Asko to be nothing more than a simple block of wood. The voice on the hill commanded Kaito to split the Asko with his hatchet.

The lion, thinking itself safe, begged Kaito to strike it gently. But Kaito, trusting in Akira's plan, swung his hatchet with all his might, splitting the lion's skull and ending its reign of terror.

Akira descended from the hill, proud of their joint success. He claimed his reward: a fine ram that Kaito had promised him. However, Kaito's wife, wise to Akira's cunning nature, suggested that they keep the ram for themselves instead of giving it to the sly jackal.

Kaito, unaware of his wife's plan, brought the ram to the field in a wicker basket with a leather sack and their loyal dog lying beside it. He called out to Akira, "Here is your reward!" but Akira, upon approaching the basket, was chased away by the snarling dog.

As Kaito returned home, he found the ram still intact within the basket. He laughed at Akira's failure and decided that they would enjoy the ram themselves after all. The jackal, enraged by his own foolishness, vowed never to aid humans again.

From that day forward, Kaito continued to work in peace, while Akira slunk away, a lesson learned about the dangers of underestimating the cunning of others.

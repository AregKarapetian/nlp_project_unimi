In a small village nestled between two great mountains, there lived a humble farmer named Kaito. He toiled from dawn till dusk, tending to his fields and oxen with dedication and care. However, his life was soon disrupted by the arrival of a fearsome lion, whose piercing roar echoed through the valleys.

The lion would appear every evening, demanding one of Kaito's precious oxen as tribute, threatening to destroy both beast and farmer if its demand was not met. Kaito, fearing for his life and livelihood, reluctantly surrendered an ox each night. He would then purchase another, ensuring that he could continue his daily labor.

One fateful evening, a cunning jackal named Jiro chanced upon Kaito as he drove home with the remaining oxen. The jackal's sharp eyes observed the farmer's predicament and devised a plan to free him from the lion's tyranny.

"Farmer Kaito," said Jiro, "I have watched you surrender an ox each night, only to buy another in the morning. Tell me, why do you not defy the lion and protect your livestock?" Kaito explained his situation, and Jiro proposed a solution: "If you promise to reward me with a sheep, I will rid you of this beast."

Kaito agreed, and Jiro instructed him on how to outwit the lion. The next day, as the farmer worked in the fields, a deep voice boomed from the nearby hill, asking who was speaking with Kaito. The lion cowered, claiming it was only "the god" speaking. But Kaito boldly declared that it was merely an Asko (a block of wood to be split). The voice demanded that Kaito take his hatchet and split the block.

The lion, still trembling, begged Kaito to strike gently. Seizing the opportunity, Kaito brought down his hatchet with all his might, splitting the lion's skull and ending its reign of terror.

Jiro descended from the hill, triumphant in his promise. "Your debt is paid, farmer," he said. "I will collect my reward tomorrow." Kaito nodded in agreement, but as he returned home, his wife whispered a mischievous plan into his ear.

The next day, Kaito presented Jiro with a wicker basket containing a leather sack with the promised ram inside. However, their clever dog had been placed beside it, and when Jiro approached to claim his prize, the canine sprang up, chasing the jackal away.

As the sun dipped below the horizon, Kaito returned to find the ram still intact in the basket. He picked it up, intending to bring it home for dinner. But as he looked into the basket, he realized that Jiro had not claimed his reward after all. The farmer's eyes narrowed, and a sly smile spread across his face.

"Ah, it seems we can enjoy this ram ourselves," he said, carrying the basket back home with a newfound sense of satisfaction. From that day forward, Kaito and his family feasted on the ram, while Jiro slunk away, vowing never to aid men again.

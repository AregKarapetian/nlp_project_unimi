In days of yore, when the great sea stretched far and wide, there lived a mighty dragon who dwelled in its depths. His wife, a gentle soul, lay bedridden, her vitality waning like the ebbing tide. The loving husband beseeched her, "My dear, what shall I fetch for you to eat?" But she remained silent as the night, her eyes cast downward.

The dragon's heart swelled with devotion, and he pleaded once more, "Just tell me, my love, and I shall bring it forthwith." She finally spoke, her voice barely above a whisper. "I desire a monkey's heart to nourish mine own."

The dragon set out on the shore, his massive form towering over the landscape. He spied a mischievous monkey perched atop a tree, its tail swishing in the breeze. The dragon called out, "Hail, shining one! Are you not afraid of falling?" The monkey replied with a grin, "No, I have no such fear."

The dragon offered the monkey a ride across the sea, promising it would find forests teeming with fruit and flowers beyond its wildest dreams. But as they reached the midpoint, the dragon dived down into the depths, leaving the monkey to wonder where he was taking him.

"Where are you going?" the monkey asked, his eyes stinging from the saltwater. The dragon replied, "My dear sir, my wife is ailing and has taken a fancy to your heart." The monkey thought within himself, "What a fool this dragon is!" He then spoke up, "Illustrious friend, why did you not tell me sooner? I left my heart on the top of that tree; take me back, and I will retrieve it for Mrs. Dragon."

The dragon returned to the shore, but the monkey tarried in coming down from its perch. The dragon implored him once more, "Hurry up, little friend! I am waiting." And so, the monkey descended, thinking to himself, "This dragon is indeed a fool!" Little did he know that he was speaking with one who would soon become the Buddha.

The great Master himself revealed later, "At this very moment, I was the monkey."

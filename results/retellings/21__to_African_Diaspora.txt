In days of yore, when the great waters roared with fury, a mighty dragon dwelled within their depths. His beloved wife's vitality waned, her radiance fading like the morning sun. The dragon, filled with compassion and concern, asked his dear one, "My love, what shall I bring to ease your suffering?" But she remained silent, her eyes downcast.

"Tell me, my love," pleaded the dragon, "and I shall fetch it for you." His wife's voice was barely a whisper. "You cannot do it; why trouble yourself?" The dragon's heart swelled with devotion. "Trust in me, and your desire shall be fulfilled."

"My heart's longing is for a monkey's heart to eat," she whispered. The dragon's brow furrowed in concern. "But the monkeys dwell in the mountain forests! How can I retrieve one of their hearts?" His wife's words were laced with resignation. "I am soon to depart this world; I know it."

The dragon set off towards the shore, his massive form breaching the surface. He spied a agile monkey perched atop a tree and called out, "Greetings, shining one! Are you not afraid of falling?" The monkey's voice was steady. "No, I have no such fear." The dragon offered, "Why confine yourself to this single tree? Cross the sea with me, and you shall find forests teeming with fruit and flowers."

The monkey hesitated, but the dragon's words were persuasive. He climbed onto the dragon's back, and they set off towards the open waters. But as they descended into the depths, the monkey cried out, "Where are we going?" The dragon's response was a gentle one. "My dear sir, my wife is ill and yearns for your heart."

The monkey's thoughts turned to his own well-being. He spoke up, "Illustrious friend, why did you not reveal this to me sooner? I left my heart on the tree; take me back, and I shall retrieve it for Mrs. Dragon." The dragon returned to the shore, awaiting the monkey's return. But as the monkey tarried, the dragon's patience wore thin. "Hurry up, little friend! I am waiting."

The monkey thought within himself, "What a fool this dragon is!" And in that moment, the great Buddha spoke to his followers, saying, "At this time, I was the monkey."

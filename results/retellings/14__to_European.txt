In the heart of the forest, where the sun dipped into the horizon and painted the sky with hues of crimson and gold, Brer Wolf waited patiently within the walls of Brer Rabbit's humble abode. The air was heavy with anticipation as Brer Rabbit, accompanied by his gentle wife and their little ones, ventured out to gather the evening's sustenance.

As the sun began its descent, Brer Rabbit returned home, his keen senses on high alert. He noticed that the front door creaked ominously on its hinges, a sound that sent shivers down his spine. His wife and children always ensured that the door was securely latched when they departed, so this anomaly piqued his curiosity.

With stealthy steps, Brer Rabbit approached the house, his eyes scanning every nook and cranny for any sign of disturbance. He peered through the crack beneath the door, but all he saw was darkness. His ears strained to detect the slightest whisper or creak, yet the silence was oppressive.

Brer Rabbit's brow furrowed in concentration as he pondered the situation. "The pot-rack on the chimney knows what transpires within," he mused to himself, "the rafters above are aware of who resides in the loft, and the bed-cord beneath the bed whispers secrets to its occupant." But Brer Rabbit was no pot-rack, rafter, or bed-cord. He would not be fooled by his own cleverness.

With a sly grin, he climbed an adjacent simmon stump and began to holler at the top of his lungs, "Heyo, house! Why don't you respond?" The house remained mute, but Brer Wolf, hiding behind the door, felt a shiver run down his spine. He was perplexed by this peculiar behavior.

Undeterred, Brer Rabbit continued to holler, his voice growing louder with each passing moment. "Heyo, house! Why don't you answer?" The silence from within the house only fueled Brer Wolf's confusion and unease.

As the standoff persisted, Brer Rabbit's laughter echoed through the forest, a sound that sent Brer Wolf scurrying for cover. With a final, exasperated cry, "Heyo, house! Why don't you talk hoarse like you've got a bad cold?" Brer Wolf, attempting to mimic the house's tone, replied in a gruff voice, "Heyo, yo'self!"

Brer Rabbit's chuckles turned into guffaws as he realized the absurdity of the situation. With a flourish, he leapt from the simmon stump and landed with a thud on the forest floor. The commotion was too much for Brer Wolf, who slunk away, defeated.

From that day forward, Brer Rabbit lived in peace, free from the harassment of his cunning foe.

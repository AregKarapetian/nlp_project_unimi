In the heart of our village, where the sun beats down upon the earth, there lived a young man who tended to his father's buffaloes. As he watched them graze, he noticed a peculiar sight. Every day at noon, a she-dog would make its way to a nearby ravine, where water pools lay hidden. The young man's curiosity was piqued, and he wondered to whom this dog belonged and what it did in the ravine.

One day, as he watched from afar, the dog arrived at the ravine and shed its skin, revealing a beautiful maiden within. She bathed in the waters, her beauty radiating like the sun itself. When she finished, she donned the dog's skin once more and departed for the village. The young man followed her, his heart filled with wonder.

As he trailed behind, he discovered which house the maiden entered and learned its owner's name. He returned to his duties, but his mind was preoccupied with thoughts of the mysterious maiden. When his parents announced that it was time for him to marry, he surprised them by declaring that he wanted a dog as his wife.

The villagers laughed at his notion, but he remained resolute. His parents, convinced that their son had the soul of a dog within him, decided to humor him. They asked if there was any particular dog he desired as his bride, and he revealed the name of the man whose house the maiden visited.

To everyone's surprise, the master of the house agreed to sell his dog to the young man for a bride price. A wedding was arranged, and the ceremony took place with great fanfare. The young man's party escorted the "bride" back to their home, where she would transform into a maiden every night.

However, her nocturnal escapades soon caught the attention of her husband, who lay in wait one evening. He seized hold of her as she attempted to leave and threw the dog skin into the fire, reducing it to ashes. The maiden remained with him, but her beauty was now even more radiant than before.

The news spread throughout the village, and people marveled at the young man's wisdom in choosing a dog as his wife. His friend Jitu, however, was not so fortunate. Enchanted by the idea of marrying a dog, he convinced his family to arrange a union with one of their own dogs.

A bride was selected, but when the vermilion was applied to her forehead, she began to growl and snarl like an animal. Despite this, Jitu's family dragged her to his home, where they forcibly anointed her with oil and turmeric. But as soon as the "bride" party set off towards their village, she broke free and chased after them.

The villagers laughed at Jitu's misfortune, and he was too ashamed to speak for days. Eventually, in a fit of despair, he took his own life by hanging himself from a tree branch.

As we sit around the fire, sharing stories of our ancestors' wisdom and folly, let us remember that sometimes it is better to listen to one's heart and trust in the mysteries of the universe. For in the end, it was not the young man who suffered for his choice but rather those who failed to understand the magic within themselves.

This tale reminds us that the world is full of wonders and that we must be cautious not to mock what we do not comprehend. As our elders would say, "A tree is known by its fruit," and in this case, the young man's decision bore fruit in ways he could never have imagined.

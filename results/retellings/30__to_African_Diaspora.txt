In a small village nestled between two great rivers, there lived a man and his wife who were both skilled tailors. They lived in harmony, their hands moving deftly as they sewed and mended garments for the community. But one day, disaster struck. The pots, glasses, and plates in their kitchen lay shattered on the floor.

The husband's eyes narrowed as he surveyed the damage. "How did this happen?" he asked his wife, his voice laced with concern. She shrugged, her hands fluttering like birds in distress. "I don't know," she replied, her voice barely above a whisper.

The husband's patience wore thin. "What do you mean you don't know? Who broke these things?" The wife's eyes flashed, and she snatched up the scissors that lay on the table. "I did it with the scissors!" she exclaimed, as if daring him to contradict her.

The husband's face darkened. He knew his wife was a clever woman, but he also knew she was stubborn. He decided to teach her a lesson, so he tied a rope around her waist and began to lower her into the well that stood nearby. "Come down, my dear," he said, his voice dripping with sarcasm. "Tell me how you broke these things."

As they descended deeper into the well, the wife's cries grew louder. But she refused to yield, insisting that it was indeed the scissors that had shattered the pots and dishes. The husband's anger simmered like a pot left unattended on the fire.

Finally, he let her down until the water lapped at her waist. "Tell me now," he growled, his patience exhausted. But she merely shook her head, her eyes flashing with defiance.

In desperation, the husband released the rope, and his wife plunged into the water. She thrashed about, but as she sank beneath the surface, her hand shot up out of the water, fingers splayed like scissors cutting through air.

The husband's face contorted in anguish. He knew he had gone too far. With a heavy heart, he reached down and pulled his wife to safety. As they emerged from the well, dripping wet and shivering with fear, the husband realized that his wife would never confess her secret. The scissors remained silent, their blades still and unmoving.

The villagers gathered around, whispering among themselves as they watched the couple. "A stubborn heart is like a stone," one of them said, shaking her head in dismay. Another added, "Sometimes, it takes more than words to reveal the truth." And so, the husband and wife returned home, their relationship forever changed by the well's dark waters.

In the heart of the forest, where the moonlight struggled to penetrate, a traveler found himself lost in the darkness. He stumbled upon a hollow tree, its trunk worn smooth by the passing of seasons, and sought refuge within it. The night air was heavy with the scent of blooming flowers as he settled into his makeshift shelter.

As the hours ticked by, the silence was broken by the sound of laughter and music. A group of forest dwellers, known for their mischievous ways, had gathered at the tree's base. Their joyous voices carried through the night air, drawing the traveler out of his hiding place. At first, he watched with trepidation, but as the elves' revelry continued, he found himself swept up in the infectious energy.

The elves, noticing the traveler's presence, welcomed him with open arms. They shared their wine and food, and soon the traveler was dancing alongside them under the starry sky. As the first light of dawn crept over the horizon, the elves bid the traveler farewell, but not before extracting a promise from him to return and join in their festivities once more.

The elves took something precious from the traveler as collateral for his promise: a wen that had grown on his forehead. The traveler, oblivious to the elves' intentions, left the tree with a spring in his step, feeling grateful for the unexpected night of merriment and the removal of his wen.

Word of the traveler's encounter spread quickly through the village, and soon he was sharing tales of his adventure with friends and family. They marveled at his good fortune and congratulated him on being cured of his wen. However, a neighbor who had been envious of the traveler's luck from the start saw an opportunity to replicate his experience.

The neighbor set out to find the hollow tree, determined to claim its magic for himself. When he finally stumbled upon it, the elves welcomed him with open arms, thinking they were reuniting with their old friend. They returned the pawned wen, but instead of removing the traveler's original wen, they stuck it onto his forehead alongside the one he already had.

The neighbor returned home in tears, his envy and greed having led to a double burden rather than a cure. The villagers looked on, using this cautionary tale to remind each other that coveting what others have can lead to more suffering than joy.

In the village of Azizi, where the sun dipped into the horizon like a ripe mango, three brothers, Kofi, Kwame, and Kojo, huddled together, their bellies growling with hunger. The rains had been relentless, and their crops were withering away like a leaf in autumn.

"Brothers," said Kofi, "let us go hunting to catch some food for our families."

"But we have no clothes!" exclaimed Kwame, his eyes scanning the horizon for any sign of game.

"And I have no shirt!" added Kojo, scratching his bare chest.

The three brothers set off into the wilderness, their footsteps quiet on the wet earth. As they walked, they stumbled upon a hare, its fur glistening with dew. They took turns firing at it, but their shots fell short.

"What shall we do?" asked Kwame, frustration etched on his face.

Kojo remembered that their godmother, Yaa, lived nearby. "Let us go to her house and ask for help," he said.

Yaa was not at home, but she answered their call from afar. "My children, go into the kitchen and choose one of the three pots hanging on the wall. Two are broken, and one has no bottom."

The brothers entered the kitchen, their eyes scanning the shelves. They chose the pot with no bottom and placed the hare inside to cook.

As they waited for the hare to finish cooking, Kofi suggested, "Let us ask Yaa if she has any walnuts in her garden."

Yaa replied, "I have three walnut trees. Two are dead, but one has never borne nuts. Knock off as many as you wish."

Kwame went to shake the barren tree, and a small nut fell onto his hat, breaking his heel.

The brothers picked up the remaining nuts and returned to Yaa's house. As they sat down to eat, Kojo exclaimed, "What shall we do with all this food?"

"Let us take it to the village of Aburi," said Kofi. "Many people are ill there, and we can give them some broth in charity."

The brothers arrived at the village and posted a notice on the town square: "Whoever is hungry may come and get some free broth!"

People from all over the village flocked to get their share of the hot meal. One stranger drank so much that he was on the brink of death.

The brothers decided to seek out three physicians to help him. The first one, a blind man named Opanin, said, "Let me examine your tongue."

The second physician, a deaf man named Nana, asked, "How are you?"

The third doctor, a dumb man named Kofi, wrote on a piece of paper: "Go to the apothecary and buy two cents' worth of something. Put it wherever you wish. He will get well when he gets well."

The brothers followed Opanin's instructions and purchased some medicine from the apothecary. They gave it to the stranger, who eventually recovered.

As they walked back to their village, Kofi said, "Brothers, we may not have clothes or food, but we have each other, and that is all that matters."

Kwame nodded in agreement, while Kojo smiled, his eyes shining with gratitude.

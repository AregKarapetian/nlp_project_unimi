In a land far, far away, a majestic lion found himself trapped in a rocky cleft, unable to free himself. A wise and agile horse, who happened to be passing by, overheard the lion's cries for help. "I'll do that," said the horse, "but you must promise not to devour me once I've set you free." The lion, desperate for escape, promised to spare the horse's life.

With a few swift kicks of his hooves, the horse managed to dislodge the rocks and set the lion free. But as soon as the lion was out, he snarled, "Now that I'm free, I'll make a meal of you!" The horse retorted, "What about our agreement? You promised not to eat me." The lion scoffed, but the horse suggested they take their dispute before a wise and cunning judge.

They chose the fox as their arbiter, and presented their case. The fox listened intently, then said, "I'll render my verdict, but first I must see how you two stood when you made your agreement." So all three of them returned to the rocky cleft, where the horse reenacted his original position. The fox instructed the lion to squeeze himself back into the narrow space, just as he had been before.

The lion pushed and squeezed, but with each attempt, he became more wedged in. Finally, he was stuck fast, unable to extricate himself. The fox declared, "Now you are exactly where you were before! It's up to the horse whether he'll help you out again." But the horse had no intention of doing so; instead, he began hurling stones at the trapped lion until it met its demise.

The fox smiled slyly, knowing that justice had been served.

In a land far away, a cunning lion was trapped in a rocky cleft. A wise and agile horse heard his cries for help and offered to free him. "I'll do that," said the horse, "but you must promise not to devour me." The lion, eager to escape, promised to spare the horse's life.

Once freed, however, the lion's true nature reasserted itself. "Now I will eat you!" he roared at the horse. But the horse stood firm, recalling their agreement. "Did we not agree that you would not devour me?" he asked. The lion sneered, but the horse suggested they seek the judgment of a wise and impartial judge.

The fox, renowned for his cunning, was chosen as the arbiter. The lion presented his case, claiming that his promise no longer bound him. But the fox proposed a test: to see how the two had stood in the rocky cleft when the horse first freed the lion. With the fox's guidance, the lion was forced to reenact their original positions, twisting and pushing himself back into the narrow space.

As the lion struggled to free himself, the fox declared, "You are now exactly as you were before! The horse may decide whether to help you again." But the horse had no intention of releasing the lion. With a sly smile, he began throwing stones at the trapped beast until it was still and silent. Indeed, the fox's cunning had led to the lion's downfall.

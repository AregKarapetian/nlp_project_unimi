In a sun-dappled clearing, Br'er Rabbit strolled along, humming a merry tune to himself. As he wandered, he noticed a shadow flitting about above him. He looked up to behold the majestic form of Mr. Hawk soaring overhead. The hawk's wings beat steadily, drawing closer with each revolution, yet Br'er Rabbit remained oblivious to the danger lurking above.

Before long, Mr. Hawk swooped down, talons extended, and snatched Br'er Rabbit from his unsuspecting path. The rabbit found himself ensnared in a vice-like grip, his breath growing short as he struggled to free himself. He cried out for mercy, but it was of no avail. His cries and pleas fell on deaf ears.

Br'er Rabbit lay still, pondering his predicament. After a moment's reflection, he spoke up, "I'm not much of a meal for one such as yourself, Mr. Hawk. You'd do better to catch some jay-birds instead." The hawk's eyes narrowed at this suggestion, and Br'er Rabbit knew he had struck a nerve.

"Ah-ah, you think you're clever, don't you, Br'er Rabbit?" Mr. Hawk said with a hint of disdain. "But I'm not so easily fooled. You'll make a fine snack for me." Br'er Rabbit's eyes darted about as he continued to plead his case. "If that's the case, then perhaps it would be best if you waited until I've had time to calm down. A wild rabbit such as myself wouldn't be very palatable."

Mr. Hawk snorted in derision, but Br'er Rabbit persisted. "Or, if you'd rather, you could wait for me to grow a bit larger. Then I'll make a more substantial meal." The hawk's expression softened slightly at this proposal.

Br'er Rabbit continued to cajole and wheedle, his words dripping with honeyed persuasion. Eventually, Mr. Hawk found himself swayed by the rabbit's arguments. "Very well," he said finally. "I'll wait for you to grow larger. But don't think you can escape me that easily!"

Br'er Rabbit, however, had no intention of growing larger or driving out partridges as promised. With a mischievous glint in his eye, he waited until Mr. Hawk let his guard down before making his escape. The hawk, left to ponder the rabbit's cunning, was forced to admit defeat once again.

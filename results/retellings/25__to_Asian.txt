In a small village nestled between two great mountains, there lived a humble couple, Ah-Ming and his wife, Mei-Ling. One day, Ah-Ming said to Mei-Ling, "Let us make some delicious yóu tiáo" – crispy fried dough sticks – for our evening meal." Mei-Ling replied, "But where shall we cook them? We have no wok." Ah-Ming suggested, "Go and borrow one from my jiaojie, my aunt by marriage. It is only a short walk away."

Mei-Ling went to her aunt's house and borrowed the wok, saying, "Here is the wok, but you must carry it back when we are done with it." Ah-Ming was pleased and they cooked the yóu tiáo together. After finishing their meal, Ah-Ming said, "Now let us go to work, both of us, and the one who speaks first shall carry the wok back to your aunt's house."

Mei-Ling began to spin silk threads on her spindle, while Ah-Ming started to weave a new pair of shoes. They worked in silence, except for the occasional sound of Mei-Ling's spinning wheel or Ah-Ming's shuttle as he wove. As they worked, a lone soldier rode by on his horse, asking a passerby if there was a skilled shoemaker in the neighborhood.

The woman pointed to their house and said, "Ah, yes! That is where Ah-Ming lives." The soldier dismounted and entered their home, saying, "Good fellow, I need you to cut a new girth for my horse. I will pay you well."

Ah-Ming made no response but continued weaving, saying only, " Zhēn zhēn" – a gentle humming sound. Mei-Ling replied with her spinning wheel's rhythmic whirring, "Ti-ti-ti." The soldier grew impatient and threatened to cut Ah-Ming's head off if he didn't comply.

Ah-Ming remained silent, still weaving, while Mei-Ling continued to spin. The soldier's anger boiled over, and he seized his sword, ready to strike. But just as he was about to bring it down, Mei-Ling cried out in distress, "Ah! Please don't harm him!" Ah-Ming exclaimed, "Now you go and carry the wok back to my aunt's house, and I will go and cut your horse's girth."

Mei-Ling hastily gathered the wok and rushed off, while Ah-Ming went to work cutting the new girth. He won the wager, and they both laughed at their cleverness in outwitting the soldier. As they sat down to enjoy another meal together, Ah-Ming smiled at Mei-Ling and said, "You see, my dear? Sometimes it's better to be patient and wait for the right moment to speak."

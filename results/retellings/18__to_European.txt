In a small village nestled between two great rivers, there lived a humble farmer named Giovanni. He toiled from dawn till dusk, tending to his land and his livestock with the utmost care. However, his life was soon disrupted by a most fearsome creature: a lion, as black as coal and with eyes that glowed like embers.

The lion would appear every evening, demanding one of Giovanni's oxen under threat of death for both beast and man. The farmer, though terrified, complied with the lion's demands, sacrificing an ox each night to placate the beast. He would then purchase another ox in the evening market, so that he might continue his work the next day.

One fateful eve, as Giovanni was driving home his single ox, a cunning jackal named Lupino approached him. "Good sir," said Lupino, "I've been watching you from afar. Every morning I see two oxen in your field, but every evening only one returns with you. Pray tell, what sorcery is this?"

Giovanni explained the lion's demands and threats, to which Lupino replied, "Fear not, good farmer. I shall free you from this beast's tyranny if you promise me a fine ram from your flock." Giovanni agreed, and Lupino devised a plan.

"Tomorrow," said Lupino, "I will call out with a disguised voice from yonder hill. When the lion arrives, you must respond that it is only an Asko, a block of wood to be split. Have your hatchet at hand, for I shall give you instructions."

Giovanni followed Lupino's plan to the letter. The next day, as he plowed with his two oxen, the lion appeared and demanded one of them. Just then, a deep voice boomed from the hill: "Farmer, who speaks with you?" Giovanni replied loudly, "It is only an Asko!" The voice ordered him to split the block of wood, and Giovanni, following Lupino's instructions, struck at the lion's skull with all his might.

The lion let out a deafening roar as it fell to the ground, its body shattered into two pieces. Lupino descended from the hill, a sly grin spreading across his face. "I have fulfilled my promise," he said. "Now, good farmer, I claim the ram you owe me."

Giovanni returned home, eager to fulfill his debt to Lupino. He told his wife of the jackal's bravery and promised her that they would give him a fine ram as payment. However, she had other plans. She packed the ram into a leather sack, laid it in a wicker basket with their faithful dog, and instructed Giovanni to bring it back if Lupino did not take it.

The next day, Giovanni placed the basket in the field and called out to Lupino, "Here is your ram!" He then returned to his work, leaving the basket unattended. However, when Lupino approached the basket, the dog leaped up, chasing the jackal away with a fierce bark. The jackal swore never to aid men again, and Giovanni, upon returning home, found the ram still untouched in the basket.

He picked it up, brought it back to their hut, and said to his wife, "It seems Lupino has no interest in our gift. We shall eat this fine ram ourselves." And so, they feasted on its tender meat, grateful for the unexpected bounty.

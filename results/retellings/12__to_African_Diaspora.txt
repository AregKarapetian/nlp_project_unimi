They had been blessed with a child, but they knew that before presenting him to the community, they must find a worthy godparent to guide him on his life's journey. The father took the child in his arms and set out into the village square, seeking someone who embodied justice and righteousness.

As he walked, he encountered a stranger, whose presence seemed both familiar and mysterious. "I seek a just person to be my child's godfather," said the farmer. The stranger replied, "Ah, but can one truly know if they are just?" The farmer was taken aback by this response and continued his search.

He met a wise woman, who was revered throughout the land for her compassion and kindness. "I have been searching for someone to be my child's godfather," said the farmer. "Are you just?" she asked, with a knowing gaze. "I don't know," she replied, "but you will find one who is." The farmer pressed on, undeterred.

As he walked through the village, he came face to face with another woman, whose presence seemed both gentle and unyielding. "I have been told that you are just," said the farmer. "Is it true?" she asked, her voice low and soothing. "Yes, I am just," she replied. "Let us baptize your child, and then I will show you what it means to be just." And so they baptized the child.

Afterwards, Death led the peasant into a vast chamber filled with an abundance of light. The farmer's eyes widened as he beheld the radiance that surrounded him. "Godmother," he asked, "what is this wondrous sight?" Death replied, "These are the lights of all souls in the world. Yours and your son's are among them." As the peasant gazed upon his own light, he knew its time was drawing to a close.

"Ah, godmother," he cried, "when my oil runs out, what will become of me?" Death's response was firm but gentle. "Then you must come with me, for I am the one who guides all souls to their final rest." The peasant implored, "May I take a little oil from my son's lamp and put it in mine?" But Death would not be swayed. "You sought a just person, and now you have found one," she said. "Go home, arrange your affairs, for I am waiting for you."

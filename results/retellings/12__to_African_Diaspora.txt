In the village of Azizi, where the sun dipped into the horizon like a ripe mango, there lived a humble farmer named Kofi and his wife, Ama. They had a child, a precious gift from the Ancestors, but they refused to have him baptized until they found a just person to be his godfather.

Kofi took their child in his arms and set out into the bustling streets of Azizi, searching for someone with a heart full of justice. As he walked, he met a wise elder, who was none other than the great Orisha, Nyame. Kofi asked him, "I seek a just person to be my child's godfather. Are you just?" Nyame replied, "Ah, but I am not sure if I am just. The path of justice is long and winding." Kofi continued his search, meeting many people along the way.

He met a woman who was as gentle as a summer breeze, but she too said, "I don't know if I am just. But go on, for you will find someone who embodies justice." Kofi pressed on, until he came to a woman with skin as dark as the night sky and eyes that shone like stars. She was Mami Wata, the goddess of life and death.

Kofi asked her, "I have been searching for a just person to be my child's godfather. Are you just?" Mami Wata replied, "Yes, I believe I am just. Let us baptize your child, and then I will show you what it means to be just." They baptized the child, and afterwards, Mami Wata led Kofi into a vast chamber filled with lights that shone like a thousand candles.

"Godmother," Kofi exclaimed, "what are all these lights?" Mami Wata replied, "These are the lights of all the souls in the world. Would you like to see, friend? This is yours and this is your son's." When Kofi saw his own light flickering out, he begged Mami Wata, "Oh, for mercy's sake, let me take a little oil from my son's lamp and put it in mine!" But Mami Wata said, "No, no, godfather. You wished to see a just person, and a just person you have found. Now go home and arrange your affairs, for I am waiting for you."
